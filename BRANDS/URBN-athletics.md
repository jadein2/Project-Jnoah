## 2. Temporary Visual Direction

The final visual identity is still open.

Until approved, use:

- Black and white base
- Minimal layouts
- Clean typography
- Strong gym photography
- Clear spacing
- Direct headlines
- Simple wordmark treatment
- No cluttered backgrounds
- No cheap sale-poster style
- No intimidating hardcore tone

The design should feel:

- Strong
- Clean
- Coach-led
- Urban
- Professional
- Beginner-friendly
- Premium but accessible

---

## 3. Visual Hierarchy

For most assets, prioritize in this order:

1. URBN Athletics
2. Main message or campaign headline
3. Built to Move Better
4. Call to action
5. Supporting detail

For rebrand assets, prioritize:

1. Urban Strong Fitness is becoming URBN Athletics
2. Better coaching and client experience. A positive community. A high standard. A better space to train in.
3. Built to Move Better
4. CTA or date/detail if needed

---

## 4. Photography Direction

Use real, strong, human gym photography when possible.

Preferred subjects:

- Coaches guiding members
- Movement assessment
- Strength training
- Small group coaching
- Members training with focus
- Clean facility shots
- On-Ramp teaching moments
- PFT / Hybrid preparation
- Real community moments

Avoid:

- Stock-looking fitness poses
- Overly dark dramatic edits
- Hardcore screaming imagery
- Random equipment-only photos with no coaching context
- Photos that make the brand feel intimidating to beginners

## 9. Design QA Checklist

Before publishing an asset, check:

- Uses URBN Athletics as the public brand
- Uses Built to Move Better when appropriate
- Does not accidentally present Urban Strong Fitness as the current brand
- Explains that the coaching, community, and standard remain the same
- Feels coach-led, structured, and clear
- Does not look like a cheap access-only gym promo
- Does not sound intimidating to beginners
- Has one clear CTA
- Uses real coaching or gym context where possible
- Keeps text short enough to read quickly
