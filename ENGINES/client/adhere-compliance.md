## Why this system exists

The single highest-leverage point in the 90-Day Coaching System is the first 14 days. Training consistency in the first 28 days is the strongest predictor of 6-month adherence, and the protective effect attenuates over time. Win the first two weeks, and downstream compliance largely takes care of itself.

This document operationalizes that finding. It is the compliance backbone for the 90-Day Coaching System and is designed to be portable across any current or future coaching offer.

ADHERE is not a motivational concept. It is a checklist with a script. If a client does not complete the framework, they do not progress to the next phase.

## The nine compliance factors

Synthesized from research on exercise adherence, habit formation, and behavior change in coaching contexts. Each factor maps to a step in the ADHERE framework.

| # | Compliance Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pre-program mindset | Dropouts are predictable before Day 1. People who already associate training with negative affect drop out at higher rates. |
| 2 | First-28-day frequency | Strongest behavioral predictor of long-term adherence. Frequency beats intensity in this window. |
| 3 | Friction reduction | Most coaches over-index on motivation. Real compliance wins come from removing friction. |
| 4 | Habit anchoring | New behaviors attached to existing routines stick faster — especially in onboarding. |
| 5 | Self-monitoring | Tracking builds awareness and implicit accountability. Consistent logging predicts adherence. |
| 6 | Minimum Effective Dose | Easier first weeks predict better outcomes. Win small, win early, then scale. |
| 7 | Co-created accountability | Ask "how do you want to be held accountable?" The act of designing the plan creates buy-in. |
| 8 | Sleep and baseline well-being | Poor sleep at intake predicts early dropout independent of motivation. Screen for it. |
| 9 | Structured weekly check-ins | Use a consistent script: Wins → Challenges → Adjustments → Next steps. |

## The ADHERE framework

Six steps. Each step has a tool. No step is skipped.

| Letter | Stage | What It Means | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Anchor baseline | Assess mindset, sleep, environment, capability. Identify existing daily anchors. | Day -7 to 0 |
| **D** | Decide minimums | Co-create non-negotiables. Set Minimum Effective Dose training and one nutrition habit. | Day -7 to 0 |
| **H** | Habit-stack | Attach each new behavior to an existing daily routine. | Day 0 to 14 |
| **E** | Engineer environment | Friction audit. Remove obstacles before relying on willpower. | Day 0 to 14 |
| **R** | Record | Daily self-monitoring system — chosen by client, simple to maintain. | Day 1 to 90 |
| **E** | Engage weekly | Structured check-in: Wins, Challenges, Adjustments, Next steps. | Day 7 to 90 |

## Phase mapping — Foundation, Build, Perform

ADHERE is not a one-time onboarding ritual. The framework operates across all three phases of the 90-Day Coaching System, with shifting emphasis.

| Phase | Days | Primary ADHERE Focus | Coach Posture |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FOUNDATION** | 1–30 | A, D, H, E — build the platform. Establish frequency. Install one nutrition habit and one sleep habit. | High-touch. Daily presence. Frame everything as discovery, not performance. |
| **BUILD** | 31–60 | R, E intensified — escalate volume and complexity. Add metrics. Introduce structured nutrition. | Medium-touch. Weekly calls. Push the edges of capability while protecting the habits already installed. |
| **PERFORM** | 61–90 | E — self-direction emerges. Client owns the system. Coach validates and challenges. | Low-touch. Bi-weekly calls. Prepare for graduation or program renewal. |

## Tool 1 — Client Intake Form

Use Day -7 to Day 0. Delivered as Google Form, Typeform, or ClickUp form. Every field maps to one ADHERE letter.

This form replaces and extends the basic Trainerize intake for all 90-Day Coaching System clients. It must be completed before the onboarding call.

### A — Anchor (baseline assessment)

- Primary goal — specific, measurable, time-bound.
- Why now? What changed in the last 90 days that brought you here?
- Previous attempts: what worked, what didn't, what got in the way.
- Current weekly schedule (Mon–Sun, by hour).
- Average sleep hours per night.
- Sleep quality, 1–10.
- Stress level today, 1–10.
- Energy level today, 1–10.
- Complete the sentence: "When I think about training, I feel ___."

### D — Decide minimums

- Maximum training sessions per week you can realistically commit to.
- Minimum sessions per week — your non-negotiable floor (typically 3).
- Preferred training days and times.
- One nutrition habit you can commit to for the next 30 days, no excuses.
- One sleep habit you can commit to for the next 30 days.

### H — Habit-stack

- List 3 existing daily anchors you never skip (e.g., morning coffee, post-dinner walk, brushing teeth at night).
- Which new habit will attach to which anchor?

### E — Engineer (environment audit)

- Where will you train? (home, gym, both)
- Top 3 obstacles to training consistently right now.
- Who shops, who cooks, and what is typically in the fridge?
- Notification setup — what currently interrupts your training time?

### R — Record

- Preferred tracking method: app, notebook, spreadsheet, photo log.
- How will you log training? Nutrition? Sleep? Weight?
- Are you willing to share your tracker with the coach weekly?

### E — Engage (accountability design)

- How do you want to be held accountable? (DM, group chat, weekly call, public post, financial stake)
- Cadence: daily, 3x/week, weekly.
- Best time of day for check-ins.
- What should the coach do if you go silent for 48 hours?

## Tool 2 — Week 1–2 SOP (The Rescue Zone)

This is where compliance is won or lost. Follow this protocol verbatim for all 90-Day Coaching System clients. Do not skip stages. Do not escalate the program.

### Day -7 to Day 0 — Pre-launch

- Send intake form (Tool 1). Set 48-hour completion deadline.
- Conduct 30-minute onboarding call. Reframe exercise as identity work, not punishment.
- Co-design accountability format using the client's intake answers.
- Send Welcome Kit: training schedule (Days 1–14), one nutrition habit, one sleep habit, link to tracker.
- Confirm Day 1 in writing.

### Days 1–7 — Frequency over intensity

- Primary goal: hit 3 training sessions. That is the only metric that matters this week.
- No nutrition tracking yet. Only the one nutrition habit from intake.
- No body metrics. No weigh-in. No progress photos.
- Daily 30-second check-in from client: thumbs-up + one line ("Slept 7 hours, mood 7/10, training done").
- Coach response: one-line acknowledgment. No advice. No coaching. Just presence.

### Days 8–14 — Layer one

- Maintain 3 sessions per week minimum.
- Activate the one nutrition habit consistently.
- Activate the one sleep habit consistently.
- Conduct a Friction Audit (call or DM): "What was hardest this week?" Fix the environment, not the client.

### End of Week 2 — Decision point

Run Tool 3 (Weekly Check-In Script). Use the outcome to set Week 3 strategy:

- **Hit 5–6 of 6 sessions:** escalate. Move into Build prep. Add second nutrition habit.
- **Hit 3–4 of 6 sessions:** hold steady. Repeat Week 2 protocol for one more week.
- **Hit 0–2 of 6 sessions:** return to onboarding. Re-run the intake. Identify what broke.

## Tool 3 — Weekly Check-In Script (90-Day Coaching System)

Run every week from Week 2 onward. 10–15 minutes. Same four steps, every time. Consistency of structure builds trust.

See check-in.md for the full 90-Day check-in section — this script is the standard for all 90-Day Coaching System clients.

### Step 1 — Acknowledge wins (3 minutes)

Ask: "What went well this week? What did you do that you're proud of?"

Reinforce the behavior, not the outcome ("You showed up Tuesday when you didn't feel like it" — not "You're crushing it"). Do not rush to challenges.

### Step 2 — Clarify challenges (4 minutes)

Ask: "What felt hard? Where did you fall short of what you wanted?"

Listen without judgment. Do not problem-solve in this step. Reflect back what you heard before moving on.

### Step 3 — Explore adjustments (5 minutes)

Ask: "What's one thing we could change to make next week easier?"

Co-create the solution. The client should speak first. Pick ONE adjustment, not three.

### Step 4 — Set next steps (2 minutes)

State: "So next week, your focus is ___. Yes?"

Confirm verbally. Send the same line in writing within 1 hour. Schedule the next check-in before ending.

## Compliance metrics — track these weekly

Five numbers. Review every Saturday business review. If any fall below target, the framework needs adjustment — not the client.

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Day 14 adherence rate | % of clients who completed 5+ of their first 6 sessions | ≥ 80% |
| 30-day retention | % still actively training at Day 30 | ≥ 85% |
| 90-day completion | % who complete the full 90-Day Coaching System | ≥ 70% |
| Habit consistency score | Average daily habit completion rate | ≥ 75% |
| Check-in completion rate | % of scheduled check-ins completed by client | ≥ 90% |

These metrics feed into /GOALS/active-goals.md once ADHERE is live on clients.
