# Post Routing Engine

Classification system for all social media content across both brands.

Source: `restart_fitness_ph_content_brain_architecture.md` + `social_media_content_design_ruleset.md`

Load this before any social post, caption, carousel, reel, or story task. Every output must be routed through this system before writing begins.

---

## The routing sequence

Before creating any content output, resolve in this order:

**Brand → Audience → Pain trigger → Message intent → C/C/V category → Content pillar → Format → One practice → One CTA → QA**

### Required routing questions

1. What brand is this for?
2. Who specifically is this for?
3. What is the one problem or belief being addressed?
4. What should the audience understand after seeing it?
5. What should they do next?
6. What format best fits the goal?
7. What brand rules must be followed?

If any of these are unclear before writing — resolve them first. Do not write blind.

## Step 1 — C/C/V Classification

Every output must be labeled as exactly one of these before writing begins.

Ask: **Is this content meant to sell, connect, or teach?**

---

### Conversion Content

**Purpose:** Move warm audience members closer to buying, booking, applying, or starting.

**Use for:**
- Pinned posts
- Offer explanation
- How to start posts
- Pricing or next-step clarity
- Testimonials and client proof
- Client interviews
- Transformation breakdowns (with process, not just result)
- Discovery call invites
- Free Assessment invites
- Program application posts
- Sales objection handling

**Must answer at least one of:**
- Who are you and what do you do?
- Who do you help?
- What does the service experience look like?
- What results or proof exist?
- How does someone start?
- What is the exact next step?

**Default CTAs:**
- Restart PH: DM "RESTART" · Book a Discovery Call · Apply for Coaching
- Urban Strong: Book your Free Assessment · Talk to a Coach

**Rules:**
- Make the next step obvious
- Do not rely on vague motivation
- Show proof when available
- Reduce confusion before asking for action
- One primary CTA only

---

### Connection Content

**Purpose:** Make the audience trust the human behind the brand.

**Use for:**
- Coach Jap lessons and reflections
- Behind-the-scenes
- Client conversations (with permission)
- Day-in-the-life
- Coaching stories
- Mistakes learned
- Brand values
- Community moments
- Gym milestones
- Member bright spots
- Real coach observations from check-ins or training

**Should make people feel:**
- This is real
- This coach understands me
- This brand is human
- This gym or program cares
- These are people I can trust

**Rules:**
- Show the person behind the brand
- Do not brag without context
- Use real observations from coaching
- Show care, standards, and personality
- Make the brand likable without becoming unserious

---

### Value Content

**Purpose:** Teach something useful before someone pays.

**Use for:**
- Myth-busting
- FAQs
- Nutrition education
- Training education
- Habit frameworks
- Beginner guides
- Simple fixes
- Checklists
- Repeated member questions
- Coaching explanations
- Practical how-to posts

**Should make the audience think:** "This helped me. I trust this coach more now."

**Rules:**
- Answer real questions people actually ask
- Teach one useful idea
- Give one practical action
- Keep the explanation simple
- Avoid sounding academic unless the topic requires evidence
- Build trust before selling

**Sources for value content:**
- Questions members ask repeatedly
- Mistakes seen in training
- Check-in patterns
- Nutrition struggles
- Beginner confusion
- Objections from prospects
- Common fitness myths

## Mandatory classification rule

Every output must be labeled internally before writing:

**Category + Pillar**

Examples:
- Conversion + Proof of Practice
- Connection + Coach Jap lesson
- Value + Nutrition Clarity
- Value + Training Clarity
- Conversion + Offer and CTA
- Connection + Community story

**If a post does not clearly fit one category:** rewrite the brief until it does.
**If a post tries to be all three:** choose the dominant category and simplify.

## Step 2 — Content Pillar Assignment

After C/C/V is set, assign one pillar.

### Pillar 1 — Proof of Practice
Shows results AND the process behind them.

Use for: client wins · testimonials · progress photos · case studies · transformation stories · before and after with process breakdown.

**Rule:** Never show a result without showing the practice that created it.

---

### Pillar 2 — System Education
Explains how the coaching process works.

Use for: frameworks · method explanation · program breakdown · onboarding flow · how the starting process works · how coaching works step by step.

**Rule:** Teach why structure matters. Don't just describe the offer.

---

### Pillar 3 — Habits and Behavior Coaching
Changes how people think and act.

Use for: decision fatigue · stress habits · cravings · comfort patterns · inconsistency · readiness issues · emotional eating patterns.

**Rule:** Make people feel seen first. Then give one repeatable action.

---

### Pillar 4 — Training Clarity and Progression
Explains training simply.

Use for: exercise education · progressions · minimum effective dose · beginner training · strength training · performance education.

**Rule:** Training content should reduce confusion, not show off complexity.

---

### Pillar 5 — Nutrition Clarity and Execution
Makes nutrition practical.

Use for: protein · fiber · meal defaults · cravings control · weekend eating · simple food rules · eating for energy.

**Rule:** Avoid extreme diets and fear-based nutrition framing.

---

### Pillar 6 — Accountability and Coaching
Positions coaching as the advantage.

Use for: check-ins · feedback · adjustments · client support · coach involvement · progress reviews · habit tracking.

**Rule:** Show that coaching creates consistency through structure and feedback — not just motivation.

---

### Pillar 7 — FAQs and Objections
Removes friction before the sale.

Use for: no time · not ready · beginner fear · price concerns · what happens first · what if I fail again · do I need experience.

**Rule:** Answer plainly. Do not sound defensive.

---

### Pillar 8 — Offer and CTA
Invites action clearly.

Use for: discovery call · free assessment · challenge registration · program application · booking reminder.

**Rule:** Sell the next step, not the entire business in one post.

## Step 3 — Format Selection

| Use | Format |
|---|---|
| Idea needs explanation · want saves · teaching a framework · breaking down a client story · handling objections | **Carousel** |
| Simple reminder · quick engagement · strong visual statement · announcement · quote or belief shift | **Single image** |
| Reach matters · emotional idea · coaching footage available · quick insight to deliver fast | **Reel** |
| Daily presence · warm audience nurturing · want replies · polls · behind-the-scenes trust | **Story** |
| Topic needs depth · proof-based · coaching lesson · minimal image | **Long caption** |

## Weekly publishing framework

### Minimum weekly system
- 1 Conversion post
- 1 Connection post
- 1 Value post
- Stories at least 5 days per week
- Daily replies, DMs, and comment engagement

### Expanded system (active lead generation weeks)
- 2 Value or educational posts
- 1 Proof post
- 1 Habit or belief-shift post
- 1 CTA or Conversion post
- Stories daily
- 1–2 Reels when reach is needed

**Story rule:** Stories do not need to be perfect. They must keep the brand present and create conversations.

**Conversation rule:** Social media is not only posting. Reply, ask questions, and move qualified conversations toward the next step.

---

## CTA selection guide

### Comment CTA
Use when the goal is DM automation, lead capture, engagement, resource delivery, or starting a conversation.

Examples: Comment GUIDE · Comment RESTART · Comment RESET · Comment PLAN · Comment START

### DM CTA
Use when the goal is a sales conversation, coaching inquiry, discovery call, or program application.

Examples: DM "RESTART" · DM COACH · DM RESET · DM ASSESSMENT

### Booking CTA
Use when the audience is warm or ready to take direct action.

Examples: Book your Free Assessment · Apply for the 6-Week Reset · Start with a Free Assessment · Talk to a Coach · Book a Discovery Call

**Rule:** One CTA per post. Match CTA to audience temperature — cold traffic gets softer CTAs, warm traffic gets direct booking CTAs.

## DM conversation flows

### Restart Fitness PH
1. Thanks for messaging. What goal are you working on right now?
2. What is the biggest thing keeping you stuck — training, nutrition, schedule, or consistency?
3. Based on that, the best next step is a discovery call so we can see what structure fits you.

### Urban Strong Fitness
1. Thanks for reaching out. What goal are you working on right now?
2. Have you trained before, or would this be your first time starting again?
3. The best next step is a Free Assessment so a coach can help you find your starting point.
