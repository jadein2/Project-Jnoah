**Phase:** 13

## Core rule — read before anything else

This engine writes copy only. It does not design the page, publish the page, or define offers.

Before drafting any page:
1. Read `/BRANDS/restart-fitness-ph.md` — voice, language rules, banned words
2. Read `/ENGINES/sales/offer-engine.md` — program names, pricing, positioning lines exactly
3. Confirm which page you're writing (see page types below)
4. Confirm the primary CTA for this page (see CTA map)
5. Apply `/GLOBAL/anti-ai-output-rules.md` and `anti-ai-copywriter` skill

**Do not invent features, program names, benefits, or claims.** Everything comes from the brand file and offer file.

## Page structures

### Homepage — /

The first page cold traffic sees. Everything here must work without brand context.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Overline** — one label, 2–4 words (e.g., "Structured Online Coaching")
2. **Hero headline** — locked: *Stop guessing. Follow a system.*
3. **Hero subheadline** — 25–35 words. Problem context → what Restart Fitness PH provides. Do not name the program here.
4. **Primary CTA button** — "Book a Discovery Call" → /start
5. **Trust signal strip** — 3–4 brief proof markers (e.g., years coaching, clients coached, cities served). No invented numbers — use only confirmed figures.
6. **The problem section** — 2–4 sentences. What most people deal with. No shame, no hype.
7. **The system introduction** — introduce The Restart Fitness System. Four pillars (Training · Nutrition · Habits · Accountability). 1–2 lines per pillar.
8. **Who it's for** — 3–5 short bullets or one paragraph. Write for the person, not for the program.
9. **Results/proof section** — client proof or outcome statements. Real results only.
10. **About the coach** — 3–5 sentences. Coach Jap. Credentials, philosophy, why this system exists.
11. **Secondary CTA section** — "Ready to stop guessing?" → Book a Discovery Call

**Word count target:** 450–650 words total across all sections.

### About / Coach Jap — /about

**Purpose:** Build trust through story and coaching philosophy.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Overline** — "The Coach"
2. **Headline** — something grounded in coaching identity, not a tagline
3. **Coach Jap story** — Who he is, how he got here, why this brand exists. 100–150 words. First person or third person — confirm with Jap. Honest, not polished PR.
4. **Coaching philosophy** — The four standards (Clarity before intensity, Fundamentals before complexity, Structure before motivation, Accountability before guesswork). Write these as natural statements, not a list of jargon.
5. **What coaching looks like with Jap** — what clients actually experience. Not features — reality.
6. **CTA** — "Work with Coach Jap → Book a Discovery Call"

**Word count target:** 350–500 words.

### Online Coaching — /coaching

**Purpose:** Convert warm visitors. Explain what coaching means here and what's available.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Overline** — "Online Coaching"
2. **Headline** — Lead with outcome, not the program name
3. **Opening paragraph** — what makes Restart Fitness PH coaching different from a workout app or random plan. 2–3 sentences.
4. **Service cards** — one card per offer tier (pull from offer-engine.md):
   - 6FIX — 6-Week Foundation Program
   - Restart-P — Program-Only Coaching
   - Restart-90 — 90-Day Coaching System (flagship)
   - Restart-Base — Full Coaching (foundational)
   - Restart-Perform — Full Coaching (performance)
   - Each card: short label → 1-sentence position → 2–3 outcomes → price (if approved for public) → CTA
5. **The process section** — 4 steps: Clarity → Structure → Coaching → Accountability. 1–2 sentences each.
6. **FAQ (optional)** — 3–5 common questions. Keep answers short and direct.
7. **CTA section** — "Find the right fit → Book a Discovery Call"

**Word count target:** 500–700 words (excluding FAQ).

### The Restart System — /our-system

**Purpose:** Educate. This page is for people who want to understand the system before booking.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Overline** — "The System"
2. **Headline** — what the system is, not the name of it
3. **Opening** — why a system beats motivation, random workouts, or challenges. 2–3 sentences.
4. **Four pillars** — Training · Nutrition · Habits · Accountability. One section each. What it is, why it matters, what it looks like in practice.
5. **The process** — Clarity → Structure → Coaching → Accountability (four-step flow). Visual-friendly — this section maps to a process block in design.
6. **What the system is NOT** — short, punchy. Counters common objections.
7. **CTA** — "Ready to follow a system? → Book a Discovery Call"

**Word count target:** 500–700 words.

---

### Results & Proof — /results

**Purpose:** Social proof. Real outcomes from real clients.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Overline** — "Results"
2. **Headline** — frames results without hype. E.g., "What happens when people follow a system."
3. **Proof entries** — client results, before/after context (not before/after photos unless provided by client with consent), transformation narratives. Each entry: name (first name or alias) · what they came in with · what changed · timeframe.
4. **Result categories** — group by outcome type if there are enough entries (strength, fat loss, consistency, lifestyle).
5. **CTA** — "Want results like this? → Book a Discovery Call"

**Word count target:** Depends on number of proof entries. Aim for quality over volume.

**Rule:** Never invent results. Never approximate numbers. Use only confirmed client data.

### Book a Discovery Call — /start

**Purpose:** Convert. This is the booking page. Copy here is minimal — the form does the work.

**Sections in order:**
1. **Headline** — direct. What happens on a Discovery Call.
2. **3–4 bullet points** — what they'll walk away with from the call.
3. **What to expect** — short paragraph. No-pressure call, 20–30 minutes, no obligation.
4. **Booking form or embed** — Calendly or Wix form.
5. **Below the form** — short objection-handling copy (optional). 1–2 lines max.

**Word count target:** 100–150 words total. Less is more here.

## Copy rules — always enforced

### Voice on web
- Professional, authoritative, clear (per brand file)
- Write for someone who just found the brand — not an insider
- No jargon, no inside language
- Every section should pass the 3-second clarity test

### Structural rules
- Lead with the outcome the visitor wants — not the program or feature
- One main idea per section
- One CTA per page (except Homepage, which may have two — one above fold, one below)
- No fake urgency ("limited spots" unless genuinely true and confirmed)
- No passive voice in headlines
- No question headlines unless they're tight and earn their space

### Word count discipline
- Headlines: 3–8 words
- Subheadlines: 15–30 words
- Body sections: 40–80 words each
- CTA copy: 3–6 words

### Banned on web pages (in addition to universal rules)
- "Don't just take our word for it" (overused)
- "Life-changing" · "transformation" as a standalone word
- "We are passionate about" anything
- "Join the family"
- "On your terms" or "at your own pace" — implies no accountability, contradicts brand

## Pre-draft checklist

Before writing any section:
- [ ] Brand file read for this session?

- [ ] Page CTA confirmed?
- [ ] Anti-AI protocol loaded?

---

## Post-draft QA checklist

- [ ] Headline leads with outcome, not program name?
- [ ] Subheadline under 35 words?
- [ ] No "tailored" anywhere?
- [ ] No banned phrases (restart as verb, busy adults, hype language)?
- [ ] Program names match offer-engine.md exactly?
- [ ] One CTA per page?
- [ ] Every section passes the 3-second clarity test?
- [ ] Word count within target?
- [ ] No invented claims or fabricated proof?
- [ ] Reads like a coach wrote it, not a copywriter who Googled "fitness coaching"?
