# 6FIX Upgrade Sequence
**Flow:** Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Restart 90
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26

---

## Upgrade Philosophy

The upgrade is not a sales pitch. It is a coaching conversation that happens naturally because the client has data, results, and momentum. The goal is to present the next logical step at the moment when the client is most open to it — end of Week 5, before the program ends.

Never lead with price. Lead with what the client still wants that they haven't achieved yet.

---

## Upgrade 1 — Phase 1 → Phase 2

### When to plant the seed: Week 5 Saturday check-in
### When to close: Week 6 final check-in

---

**Week 5 Seed Script (DM or check-in conversation)**

> "You're in the last week. You've built something real over the past 5 weeks — the habits are starting to run on their own, your food decisions are cleaner, and you're moving consistently. That's not nothing. That's actually hard to do.
>
> Phase 1 was always about awareness and installation. Phase 2 is where we take what you've built and turn it into your personal system — individualized nutrition targets, structured training 2–3 times a week, and a higher level of coaching support.
>
> I want you to think about one thing before we talk on Saturday: what's still not working? What's the one thing Phase 1 didn't fully solve for you? That's exactly what Phase 2 is designed for."

---

**Week 6 Upgrade Conversation (Saturday final check-in)**

Step 1 — Complete the graduation review first. Full wins, challenges, reflection. Do not lead with the upgrade.

Step 2 — Ask the gap question:
> "Now that it's done — what's still not where you want it? Be honest."

Let them answer. Listen. What they say next is your Phase 2 pitch, in their own words.

Step 3 — Connect their gap to Phase 2:
> "That's exactly what Phase 2 addresses. Phase 1 gave you the awareness. Phase 2 builds the engine — your own training program, your specific nutrition targets, and a system that doesn't stop when the cohort does."

Step 4 — Present the offer simply:
> "Phase 2 is another 6 weeks. Two to three training sessions a week, your own meal plan built to your numbers, and weekly coaching support. The investment is [PHP amount]. It starts [start date]."

Step 5 — Let them sit with it. Do not rush:
> "You don't need to decide right now. But I want you to know that the people who see the biggest long-term results are the ones who keep the structure going after Phase 1. Think about it and let me know by [date — 3 days after]."

---

**Phase 1 → Phase 2 DM Follow-Up (send Day 42 or Day 43)**

> "Hey [name] — wanted to check in after yesterday. How are you feeling now that Phase 1 is done?
>
> If you're thinking about Phase 2, I have [X] spots open for the next cohort starting [date]. Phase 2 is where the training actually kicks in — structured sessions, your own nutrition targets, and a real system built around your schedule.
>
> DM me "PHASE2" and I'll send you the details."

---

**Objection Handling — Phase 1 → Phase 2**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I need a break first." | "That's fair. Phase 2 doesn't start for [X weeks]. Take the break — but don't let the habits fade. Let's schedule a check-in before the next cohort opens." |
| "I can't afford it right now." | "Understood. What I'd suggest is this: keep the Phase 1 system running on your own for the next 30 days. If you hit your targets, you'll know the structure works. If you drift, you'll know you need the accountability. Either way, the door stays open." |
| "I'm not sure I'm ready for training." | "That's what Week 1 of Phase 2 is for — we assess exactly where you are and start from there. Nobody walks into Phase 2 expected to be ready. The first week is a reset, not a test." |
| "I'll think about it." | "Take your time. I'll follow up in [3 days]. In the meantime, keep the habits going — that's the most important thing." |

## Upgrade 2 — Phase 2 → Restart 90

### When to plant: Phase 2 Week 5
### When to close: Phase 2 Week 6 graduation

---

**Week 5 Seed Script**

> "You're one week from finishing 12 weeks of structured coaching. That's serious work. Most people never get here.
>
> I want to put something on your radar: Restart 90 is the full-system version of what we've been doing. It's a 90-day program built for people who've already proven they can follow a structure — which you have.
>
> The difference is performance. Restart 90 is where we push your body composition further, bring in precision nutrition, and work toward a specific goal — whether that's a fitness event, a body composition target, or building a level of fitness you've never had.
>
> Think about what you want for the next 90 days. We'll talk about it in your final check-in."

---

**Phase 2 Graduation Upgrade Script**

> "You've done two phases. That's 12 weeks of building a system that works for your actual life. Here's what I see: your habits are automatic, your training is progressing, your nutrition is structured. You've proven the system works.
>
> Restart 90 is the logical next step for someone at your level. It's not for beginners — it's for people who've already built the base and want to go somewhere specific with it. Three months. Precision. A defined outcome.
>
> What's the one physical goal you've never achieved that you still want? That's what Restart 90 is for."

## Referral System — Bring Someone Into The System

**When to introduce:** Week 3 of Phase 1 (client is seeing early results and is most enthusiastic)

**Script:**

> "Quick thing — if you know someone who keeps starting over, who needs structure and accountability, who's been saying 'I'll start Monday' for months — this is the program for them.
>
> If they join because of you, you get [coaching credit / Phase 2 discount]. They get [group discount / buddy rate].
>
> Just send them my way. DM me their name and I'll reach out."

**Referral reward options:**
- ₱500 coaching credit toward Phase 2
- 10% off Phase 2 when joining as a buddy pair
- One free check-in call for the referrer

**Who to invite:**
- People who keep restarting
- People who say they don't have time
- People overwhelmed by fitness information
- People who've tried everything but haven't had a coach
