## Marketing Philosophy

6FIX marketing has one job before a cohort opens: make the right person raise their hand. Not everyone. The person who keeps restarting, feels overwhelmed, and needs a structured system — not motivation.

Everything else is noise.

The lead pipeline is simple: content builds trust → DM "6FIX" → free onboarding day converts → enrollment closes. All content either feeds the pipeline or deepens retention for active clients. Nothing is posted without a purpose.

## Content Strategy — Three Content Types

All 6FIX social content falls into one of three categories:

**Type 1 — Acquisition (cold audience)**
Purpose: Reach people who don't know Jap yet. Make them recognize themselves in the content.
Formats: Reels, carousel posts, quote graphics
Tone: "Is this you?" — relatable pain, not inspiration

**Type 2 — Authority (warm audience)**
Purpose: Build trust with people who follow but haven't committed. Show the system works.
Formats: Educational posts, before/after testimonials, coach knowledge posts
Tone: "Here's what I know" — competence, not hype

**Type 3 — Retention (active clients)**
Purpose: Keep current cohort clients connected and accountable.
Formats: Daily Stories, poll-based accountability, progress shoutouts
Tone: "I see you" — community, recognition, presence

**Ratio during an active cohort:** 40% acquisition / 30% authority / 30% retention

## Active Cycle Content Calendar (6 weeks, during cohort)

Batch this before the cohort starts. Post it on schedule. Adjust Stories in real time.

### Daily Stories (3–5 per day, active cycle only)
- Morning: daily habit reminder or coaching thought (1 slide)
- Midday: poll-based accountability ("Did you log your meals today? Yes / Not yet")
- Evening: progress prompt or win share ("What was your win today?")
- Optional: shoutout a client win (with permission)

### Weekly Reel (1 per week)
Rotate between: coaching content, client progress (with permission), behind-the-scenes coaching process, "myth vs. reality" format.

## Content Batching Workflow (Solo Operator)

To run this as a one-person operation, batch content in two sessions per cohort:

**Session 1 — 2 weeks before cohort start (3–4 hours)**
- Create all 14 pre-launch posts (captions + graphics)
- Create all 6 weekly theme carousels for the active cycle
- Create all 6 weekly reels (or plan them and shoot in 1 session)
- Schedule via Meta Business Suite or Buffer

**Session 2 — Week 1 of cohort (1–2 hours)**
- Create post-cohort content (graduation posts, next cohort announcement)
- Schedule post-cohort posts for Days +1 through +7

**Daily during cohort (15 minutes max):**
- Post 3–5 Stories (real-time, low-fi, coach voice)
- Respond to DMs
- Check Trainerize for client activity

## Key Messaging Rules (apply to all content)

- Never use "restart" as a verb in public copy
- Never say "busy adults" or "busy people" as a headline label — use "people"
- Always put "RESTART" in quotes when used as a DM CTA
- Lead with the outcome or the pain — not the program name
- No AI-sounding language: no "elevate," "unlock," "embark," "leverage," "vibrant"
- No hardcore fitness aesthetic — calm authority, not hype
- Proof over promise — client stories beat feature lists
