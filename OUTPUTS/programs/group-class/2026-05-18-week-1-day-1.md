------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 1 — MONDAY — LOWER BODY STRENGTH + METCON
------------------------------------------------------------

Stimulus Tag:
Intensity: Moderate | Primary System: Strength + Mixed | CNS Load: Medium
Expected RPE: 7–8 | Recovery Demand: Moderate

{Instructions}
Today is a squat day. The strength piece builds lower body capacity. The metcon is short and punchy — it should feel uncomfortable but controlled. Coach brief before each block. Scale the load, never the intention.

{Warm Up}
(Increase Core Temperature and Heart Rate)

{General Warm Up}
- Bike or row easy — 3 min
- Jumping jack — 20 reps
- Leg swing forward and back — 10 each leg

{Specific Warm Up}
- Bodyweight squat — 10 reps
- Goblet squat — 10 reps (light)
- Back squat with empty bar — 5 reps
- Back squat build — 3 reps @ 50% / 3 reps @ 65% / 2 reps @ 75%

{Strength}
Back Squat 5 x 5 sets

Rest 2–3 min between sets. Load is athlete-specific. Cue: chest tall, knees track, full depth.

{Metcon}
AMRAP 10

10 Cal Assault Bike
10 Goblet Squat 24/16 kg
10 Box Step-Up 24/20 in

Target: 4–5 rounds for most athletes. Pace is sustainable — not redline.

{Accessory}
Nordic curl or hamstring curl — 3 x 8
Side plank — 3 x 30 sec each side

{Scaling and Weight}

Foundations:
- Back squat: goblet squat with KB/DB at moderate load
- Goblet squat: 12/8 kg
- Box step-up: 16/12 in or bodyweight step-up

Fitness:
- Back squat: 60–70% of 1RM or RPE 7
- Goblet squat: 20/12 kg
- Box step-up: 20/16 in

Advance:
- Back squat: 80%+ of 1RM or RPE 8–9
- Goblet squat: 32/24 kg
- Box step-up: 24/20 in weighted if available

{Mobility}
- Pigeon pose — 60 sec each side
- Standing quad stretch — 45 sec each side
- Downward dog calf stretch — 5 slow reps each side

{Coach Notes}
Cue depth on the back squat — this is the first week so quality matters more than load. In the metcon, watch for athletes using the bike as rest. It should be a push. Announce the target round count before the AMRAP starts so athletes have a benchmark. Takeaway for athletes: the weight you used today is your Week 1 baseline — write it down. Action item: increase squat load next Monday if RPE felt below 7.
