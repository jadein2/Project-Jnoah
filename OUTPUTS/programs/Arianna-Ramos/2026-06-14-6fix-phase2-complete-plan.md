## PCOS note (program design)

PCOS commonly comes with insulin resistance, which means fat loss is often slower and more stubborn — that's biology, not effort, so manage expectations and celebrate non-scale wins. The good news: the levers that help PCOS most are exactly what this plan does — **higher protein, controlled carbs, strength training, and steps.** A protein-forward, lower-carb split (below) suits her well. Keep fat at a sane minimum despite the 60/30/10 target.

## Training — 6-week home progression (2x/week, ~30 min)

Built short and efficient. Two full-body-leaning sessions (**A Lower-bias, B Upper-bias**); each ~30 min. One short run folded in when time allows. On a 1-session week (her fallback), do **Session A** — never skip the strength stimulus.

| Week | Session A (Lower-bias, ~30 min) | Session B (Upper-bias, ~30 min) | Run (optional, time permitting) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Movement screen: BW squat 3×10 · RDL 3×10 · lunge 2×10/leg · plank 3×30s | Push-up 3×max · band/DB row 3×12 · dead bug 3×10 | 2.5 km easy RPE 5–6 |
| 2 | BW squat 3×15 · RDL 3×12 · glute bridge 3×20 · plank 3×40s | Incline push-up 3×12 · row 3×12 · pike push-up 3×8 | 3 km easy |
| 3 | Tempo squat 4×10 · single-leg RDL 3×8/leg · reverse lunge 3×10/leg | Push-up 4×8 · row 4×10 · band pull-apart 3×15 | run/walk 3 km |
| 4 | Squat 3×15 · RDL 3×12 · split squat 3×10/leg (deload) | Push-up 3×12 · row 3×12 · plank 3×45s | 3 km + **mid-program review** |
| 5 | Tempo squat + pulse 4×12 · single-leg hip thrust 3×10/leg | Decline push-up 4×8 · row 4×8 slow · hollow hold 3×20s | tempo 4×400m |
| 6 | Client picks + beats a number | Client-designed (you approve) | 5 km challenge (optional) |

Warm-up 5 min, rest 60s (keeps it short + dense), cooldown 5 min.

**Time-efficiency tactics (her real barrier):** pre-schedule both sessions on her off-duty days at the start of each week; 30-min cap (supersetting A1/A2 keeps it tight); "1 session is better than 0" — the floor protects the habit on chaotic weeks.
