DIAGNOSTIC NOTES
- Beginner profile — no baseline strength or capacity on file

- If any movement causes sharp pain — stop and skip it. Note it in your log.

------------------------------------------------------------
DAY 1 — FULL BODY FOUNDATION
------------------------------------------------------------

Stimulus Tag:
Intensity: Low | Primary System: Strength + Skill | CNS Load: Low
Expected RPE: 5–6 | Recovery Demand: Light

{Instructions}
Establish your squat, hinge, and press patterns at controlled load. Every rep is a practice rep. No rushing. If you feel like you could do more — good. Save it. We build from here.

{Warm Up}
(Increase Core Temperature and Heart Rate)

{General Warm Up}
- Bike or treadmill easy — 5 min
- Jumping jack — 20 reps
- Hip circle — 10 each direction

{Specific Warm Up}
- Bodyweight squat — 10 reps slow
- Romanian deadlift with empty bar — 10 reps
- Push-up — 8 reps
- Hollow body hold — 20 sec

{Strength}
Goblet Squat 10 x 3 sets
Romanian Deadlift 10 x 3 sets
DB Overhead Press 10 x 3 sets
DB Bent-Over Row 10 ea x 3 sets

Rest 90 sec between sets. Keep the same weight across all sets for Week 1. Only increase next session if RPE felt below 5.

{Conditioning}
For Time — 10 min cap

400m Run or 500m Row
15 Goblet Squat (same weight as strength block)
10 Push-Up
15 Goblet Squat
10 Push-Up

Target: under 9 min. Move at a pace you can maintain — not a sprint.

{Accessory}
Plank hold — 3 x 30 sec
Glute bridge — 3 x 12 (hold 2 sec at top)

{Replacement / Scaling Options}
- No rower: 400m walk/jog or 2 min bike
- No dumbbells: use barbell or kettlebell
- Push-up too hard: elevate hands on bench

{Mobility}
- Hip flexor stretch — 45 sec each side
- Seated hamstring stretch — 45 sec each side
- Child's pose — 60 sec

{Coach Notes}
This is Session 1. The goal is to finish, not to crush it. Note your weights and how each movement felt. Bring those notes to Session 2. One action for next session: increase goblet squat weight by 2–4 kg if today felt easy. Log your RPE after the conditioning piece.
