# 6FIX Phase 2 — Personal System Build
**Program:** 6FIX — Restart Fitness PH
**Duration:** 6 weeks (Days 43–84)
**Training:** 2–3 sessions/week, mid-to-high intensity
**Prerequisite:** Phase 1 completion
**Last updated:** 2026-05-26

---

## Phase 2 Identity

Phase 1 built awareness. Phase 2 builds the system.

Phase 1 clients who showed up, logged consistently, and completed all 6 weeks have proven one thing: they can follow a structure. Phase 2 takes that compliance and converts it into a personal operating system — individualized nutrition, intentional training, and advanced habit architecture that survives real life.

**Core message:** You no longer need motivation. You need structure.

**What changes from Phase 1:**
- Training becomes structured and progressive (not just movement recommendations)
- Nutrition moves from habit-based to individualized targets
- Habits shift from installation to automation
- Accountability moves from high-touch to medium-touch
- The client begins to own the system, not just follow it

---

## Phase 2 Non-Negotiables

- Minimum 2 training sessions per week (3 is the target)
- Individual protein target hit daily
- Meal plan followed 5 of 7 days minimum
- Weekly check-in completed every week
- Progressive overload applied — at least one metric improves each week

---

## Phase 2 Weekly Structure Overview

| Week | Theme | Training Focus | Nutrition Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Reset and Rebuild | Movement screening, baseline lifts | Individual calorie + protein targets set |
| Week 2 | Foundation Strength | Basic compound movements | Meal plan built around targets |
| Week 3 | Load Introduction | Progressive overload begins | Protein distribution refined |
| Week 4 | Mid-Program Review | Volume increase | Portions adjusted to data |
| Week 5 | Performance Push | Intensity peak | Nutrient timing introduced |
| Week 6 | Autonomy and Graduation | Client-designed session | Client-designed meal day |

## Week-by-Week Delivery

---

### WEEK 1 — Reset and Rebuild
**Theme:** Build from what Phase 1 installed. Move with intention.
**ADHERE focus:** E (engage) — transition check-in, reset baselines.

**Coach posture:** Medium-touch. Re-establish connection. Set expectations for Phase 2.

**Training (2 sessions this week):**

Session A — Movement Assessment
- Bodyweight squat: 3×10 (assess depth, knee tracking, pain)
- Hip hinge (Romanian deadlift with no weight): 3×10
- Push-up: 3×max (or wall/knee variation)
- Walking lunge: 2×10 each leg
- Plank: 3×30 seconds
- Rest 60–90 seconds between sets
- Goal: identify limitations, not perform

Session B — Active Recovery / Low Intensity
- 20–30 minute walk or light jog
- 10 minutes mobility (hip flexors, thoracic spine, hamstrings)
- 5–10 minutes breathing + stretching

**Nutrition:**
- Run ADHERE intake nutrition assessment (if not done from Phase 1)
- Calculate individual TDEE (Mifflin-St Jeor)
- Set Phase 2 calorie target (10% deficit default, 5% if stress/sleep concern)
- Assign protein target (1.8–2.2g per kg bodyweight)
- Keep Week 1 nutrition as carryover from Phase 1 meal plan while new plan is built

**Habits this week:**
- Continue Phase 1 top 3 habits
- Add: protein target awareness (not full tracking — just awareness of whether protein is hit)
- Morning routine audit: what does the first 30 minutes of the day look like?

**Weekly check-in (Saturday):**
- Weight + waist
- Movement assessment notes
- Energy levels this week
- What habit from Phase 1 felt most automatic?

---

### WEEK 2 — Foundation Strength
**Theme:** Build the structure. Every rep is practice.

**Training (2–3 sessions this week):**

Session A — Lower Body
- Goblet squat or barbell back squat: 3×8–10
- Romanian deadlift: 3×10
- Walking lunges: 3×12 each leg
- Glute bridge or hip thrust: 3×15
- Calf raises: 2×20
- Rest 90 seconds between sets

Session B — Upper Body
- Push-up (weighted or regular): 3×10–12
- Dumbbell row: 3×10 each arm
- Dumbbell overhead press or pike push-up: 3×10
- Lat pulldown or assisted pull-up: 3×10
- Face pulls or band pull-apart: 3×15
- Rest 90 seconds between sets

Session C (optional) — Full Body / Active
- 15–20 minute cardio (row, bike, jog, brisk walk)
- 2 rounds: 10 goblet squats + 10 push-ups + 10 rows + 10 hip hinges
- 5 minutes cool-down mobility

**Nutrition:**
- Full individual meal plan activated this week
- Daily protein target assigned and tracked
- Meal structure: 3 meals + 1–2 snacks, protein at every meal
- Rice/starch portions set to training day vs. rest day

**Habits this week:**
- Protein tracking begins (app or manual count)
- Evening prep habit: 10-minute next-day food prep before bed
- Post-session habit: protein meal or shake within 45 minutes of training

**Weekly check-in (Saturday):**
- Weight + waist
- Training log: sessions completed, how they felt, any pain/discomfort
- Protein hit rate: how many days did you hit your target?

---

### WEEK 3 — Load Introduction
**Theme:** Progressive overload starts now. More than last week — always.

**Training (3 sessions this week):**

Session A — Lower Body (increased load)
- Barbell back squat or goblet squat: 4×8 (add weight vs. Week 2)
- Romanian deadlift: 4×8 (add weight vs. Week 2)
- Reverse lunge: 3×10 each leg
- Hip thrust: 4×12
- Leg curl (machine or Nordic): 3×10
- Rest 90–120 seconds

Session B — Upper Body (increased load)
- Bench press or push-up variation: 4×8 (add weight or difficulty)
- Barbell or dumbbell row: 4×8 each
- Shoulder press: 3×10
- Chin-up or lat pulldown: 3×8–10
- Tricep dip or pushdown: 3×12
- Rest 90–120 seconds

Session C — Conditioning
- 5-minute warm-up (row, bike, or jog)
- 4 rounds: 200m run or 250m row + 10 burpees + 15 air squats
- 5-minute cool-down + stretching

**Nutrition:**
- Portion review based on Week 2 data
- Introduce pre-training carb strategy (rice or fruit 60–90 minutes before session)
- Post-training protein confirmed
- Water target: 2–3 liters per day minimum

**Habits this week:**
- Sleep audit: 7+ hours target, same wake time daily
- Stress log: 1–10 rating at end of each day (takes 5 seconds — builds self-awareness)
- Progressive overload log: client records weights used each session

**Weekly check-in (Saturday):**
- Weight + waist
- Training log with weights used
- Energy during sessions: 1–10
- Protein adherence this week

---

### WEEK 4 — Mid-Program Review
**Theme:** Data drives adjustments. Look at what the numbers say, then make one change.

**Training (3 sessions this week):**

Session A — Lower Body (deload volume, maintain intensity)
- Squat: 3×8 (same weight as Week 3 or slightly less)
- Romanian deadlift: 3×8
- Bulgarian split squat: 3×8 each leg
- Hip thrust: 3×12
- Rest 90 seconds

Session B — Upper Body (deload volume, maintain intensity)
- Bench or push-up: 3×8
- Row: 3×8
- Overhead press: 3×8
- Pull-up or pulldown: 3×8
- Rest 90 seconds

Session C — Active Recovery
- 30-minute walk, jog, or light swim
- 10 minutes full-body mobility
- Optional: yoga or stretching session

**Nutrition:**
- Mid-program review: compare Week 1 vs. Week 4 weight trend
- If progress stalled: review weekend adherence, liquid calories, portion creep
- If progress strong: maintain current plan, no changes needed
- Make ONE adjustment — not a full reset

**Habits this week:**
- Review Phase 1 top 3 habits: are they still running automatically?
- Add one new habit if Phase 1 habits are solid (examples: 10k steps daily, daily protein shake, morning mobility routine)
- Weekend rules review: are the 2 weekend rules working?

**Mid-program check-in (full review — 20 minutes):**
- 4 weeks of data reviewed together
- Weight trend, habit consistency, training compliance
- Set Phase 2 final 2 weeks direction: escalate, hold, or adjust

---

### WEEK 5 — Performance Push
**Theme:** You've built the base. Now push the edges.

**Training (3 sessions this week):**

Session A — Lower Body (peak week)
- Squat: 4×6 (heaviest yet)
- Romanian deadlift: 4×6
- Walking lunge: 3×12 each leg (weighted)
- Hip thrust: 4×10 (heaviest yet)
- Leg press: 3×12 (if available)
- Rest 2 minutes between heavy sets

Session B — Upper Body (peak week)
- Bench press or push-up: 4×6 (heaviest yet)
- Barbell or dumbbell row: 4×6
- Shoulder press: 4×8
- Pull-up or lat pulldown: 4×8
- Superset: bicep curl + tricep pushdown 3×12
- Rest 2 minutes between heavy sets

Session C — High Intensity Conditioning
- 5-minute warm-up
- 5 rounds: 400m run or 500m row + 15 wall balls or goblet squats + 10 push-ups
- 5-minute cool-down

**Nutrition:**
- Introduce nutrient timing actively: carbs before training, protein after
- Review satiety — is the client eating enough to support training?
- Increase calories slightly if energy is crashing mid-training (add 150–200 kcal from carbs)

**Habits this week:**
- Performance journaling: after each session, one line — "What was hard? What improved?"
- Sleep protection: this week is the most demanding — 7–8 hours is non-negotiable
- Recovery: at least 1 dedicated mobility or rest day between hard sessions

**Upgrade conversation (Week 5 check-in):**
"You're in the last week. Let's talk about what comes after Phase 2 — because what you've built here is the foundation for something bigger."

### WEEK 6 — Autonomy and Graduation
**Theme:** You own this now. The system runs without me.

**Training (2–3 sessions — client designs one):**

Session A — Coach-prescribed (repeat of a favorite week)
- Client chooses Lower or Upper from previous weeks
- Load: match Week 5 or attempt a personal best on one lift

Session B — Client-designed session
- Client plans their own session based on what they've learned
- Coach reviews and approves
- Goal: demonstrate they can program for themselves

Session C (optional) — Active / Celebration
- 5km run or walk challenge
- Any physical activity the client enjoys — their choice

**Nutrition:**
- Final week: client writes their own 7-day meal plan
- Coach reviews and adjusts if needed
- Goal: demonstrate they can plan independently

**Habits — graduation audit:**
- List all habits installed across Phase 1 + Phase 2
- Mark: Automatic / Needs maintenance / Not sticking
- Keep the automatic ones. Drop anything that's not sticking — simplify the system.

**Final check-in (full graduation review — 30 minutes):**
- Final weight + waist + photos
- Training log summary: sessions completed across 12 weeks
- Top 3 habits that changed everything
- One honest reflection: "What was harder than expected? What was easier?"
- Watch commitment video together (if client is comfortable)
- 30-day continuation plan set
- Upgrade offer: Restart 90 or continue Phase 2 monthly

**Key message:**
"The challenge ends. Your system doesn't."

## Phase 2 Upgrade Path

After Phase 2 completion, three paths:

1. **Restart 90** — Full 90-day transformation system. The natural next step for clients who want higher performance, body composition goals, or a fitness event target (run, HYROX, etc.)
2. **Phase 2 Monthly Continuation** — Ongoing Phase 2-style coaching on a monthly retainer. For clients who want to maintain structure without a new program.
3. **Graduate and self-coach** — Client has the system. They run it independently. Door stays open.
