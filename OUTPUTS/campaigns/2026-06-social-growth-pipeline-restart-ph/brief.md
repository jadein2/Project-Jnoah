## 2. Target Audience

**Primary segment:** Cold-to-warm Instagram and Facebook followers, ages 28–45, in the Philippines or Filipino diaspora.

They're people who've tried working out on their own, bought programs they didn't finish, or watched fitness content for months without actually changing. They follow fitness accounts. They know they should be doing something. But they don't have a clear plan, a coach watching them, or a system that fits real life. They don't think they need a coach — yet.

**Pain points:**
- Random workouts with no real progress
- Starting and stopping (program-hopping)
- Unsure if they're doing the right things for their goal
- Nutrition confusion — too much conflicting information
- Motivation runs out fast; nothing holds them accountable
- Feel like fitness content is made for people already in shape

**Emotional drivers:**  
They want to feel capable and in control. They want to stop second-guessing. They want to trust the process — they just haven't found a process worth trusting yet.

**Where they are:**  
Instagram Reels, Facebook feeds, YouTube fitness rabbit holes. They consume. They don't comment much. They DM when something clicks.

**Buying stage:** Unaware to early consideration. The campaign moves them from passive follower → engaged follower → DM initiator.

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Instagram (Primary)

**Why:** Highest visual and discovery potential. Reels reach cold audiences. Carousels get saves from warm audiences. Stories nurture daily. The DM "RESTART" CTA lives naturally in IG.

| Format | Role | C/C/V | Frequency |

**Effort:** Medium. Primarily adapts IG content + 2–3 standalone FB posts per week.

## 5. The Daily Content System (The 3+1 Framework)

This is the operational core of the campaign. Every day runs the same skeleton:

```
STORY 1 — Morning (7–9am)
  → Value: Quick tip, system concept, myth-bust, one actionable idea
  → Format: Text card or talking head (15–30 sec)

STORY 2 — Midday (12–2pm)
  → Connection: Real coaching moment, behind-scenes, training observation
  → Format: Photo, video clip, or text — personal, not polished

STORY 3 — Evening (6–8pm)
  → CTA: Soft push to DM "RESTART", client result teaser, or coaching insight with offer tie-in
  → Format: Text card or short clip — 1 clear action at the end

FEED POST — Any time (optimal: 6–8pm for Filipino audience)
  → Follows C/C/V weekly rotation
  → One idea. One CTA.
```

**Why 3 stories + 1 post?**  
Stories keep you visible daily in followers' feeds without requiring feed-level production every 8 hours. They build the relationship — the habit of seeing Coach Jap consistently. The feed post is the anchor. Stories are the conversation.

Every post and story belongs to one of five pillars. This prevents the feed from becoming a single-topic channel and ensures the algorithm sees topic variety — which helps reach.

| **Proof** | Client results, transformations, before/after habits (not just physique) | Conversion |
| **Coach Jap** | Philosophy, personal training approach, why he coaches this way | Connection / Conversion |

**Weekly pillar rotation (suggestion):**

- Tue: Real Coaching

## 10. DM "RESTART" Pipeline

When someone DMs "RESTART" (or any variation — "I want to know more," "how does this work," "interested in coaching"):

**Step 1 — Acknowledge immediately (within 1–2 hours)**
> "Hey! Thanks for reaching out. Before I share anything — what's your main goal right now, and what have you tried before?"

*This is a qualifying question, not a pitch. It also starts a real conversation.*

**Step 2 — Qualify**
Listen for: goal clarity, previous attempts, current schedule, commitment signals. 

Red flags that need more conversation: vague goal, price-first question, no commitment signal.  
Green lights to move forward: specific goal, prior attempt that failed (they know they need help), time-available answer.

**Step 3 — Introduce the system briefly**
> "The way I work is through The Restart Fitness System — it covers training, nutrition, habits, and accountability. It's not a random plan. Everything is built around your starting point, your schedule, and your goal. The flagship program is 90 days."

Keep this short. One paragraph. No bullet lists in a DM.

**Step 4 — Offer the Discovery Call**
> "I do a 20-minute Discovery Call to figure out if this is the right fit for you and what your starting point actually looks like. No pressure — it's just a conversation. Want to book one?"

**Step 5 — Book**
Send the booking link. If they hesitate, address the specific objection once — then let them decide.

**Step 6 — Pre-call prep**
Send a brief confirmation message 24 hours before the call with: what to expect, what to think about beforehand (goal, schedule, current routine). This increases show-up rate.

## 12. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Story fatigue — 3/day feels like too much to produce | Batch-create 1 week of stories at a time. Morning story = repurpose a line from the feed post. Midday = a real photo from training. Evening = text card. Low production, high consistency. |
| Feed post goes up inconsistently | Schedule posts in advance (Meta Business Suite or Buffer). Build 7-day content blocks on Sundays. Never be 0 days ahead. |
| DMs sit unanswered for hours | Set a morning and evening DM check time. Even a 2-line reply within 2 hours keeps the pipeline alive. |
| Reel underperforms on reach | Don't panic and pivot. Give it 3–5 days before reading results. Test different hooks. One underperforming Reel is data, not failure. |
| Campaign loses steam in Week 3 | Week 3 is proof week — the easiest week to produce (client content, results). If you have no client results to show yet, use transformation of mindset / habit wins, not just physique. |
