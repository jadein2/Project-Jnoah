## CAR-01

**Asset ID:** CAR-01

---

### Cover (Slide 1) — Hook

**Headline:** The 6-week structure that changes what's possible

**Subtext:** (none — let the headline carry it)

**Design notes:** Dark charcoal background. Headline in Cormorant Garamond Italic, large. Orange accent line or dot. Swipe prompt bottom-right: "Swipe →" in DM Sans 300.

---

### Slide 2 — The Problem

**Headline:** Most programs are built for the first two weeks

**Body:** The structure is tightest at the start — when motivation is high and everything is new. By week 3, the structure is gone. And so are most people.

**Design notes:** Off-white background. DM Sans body text. No image needed — clean, readable.

---

### Slide 3 — Why It Happens

**Headline:** It's not a discipline problem

**Body:** When there's no check-in system, no coach watching the data, no mechanism for when things get hard — the program ends where your motivation does.

That's a design problem. Not a personal one.

**Design notes:** Off-white. Single idea per slide — don't crowd this one.

---

### Slide 4 — What 6 Weeks Actually Does

**Headline:** Six weeks is specific for a reason

**Body:** Not 4 weeks. Not 12.

Six weeks is long enough to install habits. Short enough to stay accountable. And it covers the exact window where most people stop — weeks 3 and 4.

**Design notes:** Could add a simple visual — horizontal timeline bar with "Week 3–4" zone marked in orange.

---

### Slide 5 — The Check-In System

**Headline:** Every week has a check-in

**Body:** Not a form you submit and forget. A real review of what happened — what was hard, what the data shows, what needs to change.

The plan adjusts based on that. Not based on what the original plan assumed your week would look like.

**Design notes:** Off-white background. Could use a simple icon (calendar or checkmark) as a small accent — not the main element.

---

### Slide 6 — The Week-by-Week

**Headline:** What each week builds

**Body:**
Week 1 — Baseline. Training load, nutrition starting point, schedule mapped.
Week 2 — First check-in. First adjustment.
Weeks 3–4 — The hard window. Coach catches what's not working before it becomes a reason to quit.
Week 5 — Habits starting to feel like defaults.
Week 6 — Phase 1 complete.

**Design notes:** List format works here. DM Sans 400 for week labels, 300 for descriptions. Keep it tight — no more than 2 lines per week.

---

### Slide 7 — Phase 2 Exists

**Headline:** Phase 1 is not where it ends

**Body:** Weeks 7–12 — Phase 2 — is available to everyone who completes Cohort 1.

So is the 90-Day Coaching System for people who want the full structure.

6FIX is the entry point. Not the whole thing.

**Design notes:** Gold accent on this slide — authority/proof tone. Slight departure from the charcoal/off-white pattern signals something different.

---

### Slide 8 — CTA

**Headline:** 8 spots. Cohort 1 starts June 22.

**Body:** Free onboarding call on June 19. No commitment at that point — just orientation and a look at how the system works.

DM me "6FIX" and I'll send you the details.

**Design notes:** Orange "6FIX" in DM Sans 500. Charcoal background, off-white text. Clean. No visual clutter on the CTA slide.

---

### Caption (IG)

"This is what 6 weeks of structured coaching actually looks like — week by week.

Not a challenge. Not a 30-day reset. A system with check-ins, adjustments, and a coach who's watching what the data shows.

8 spots. Free onboarding call June 19. DM me '6FIX' for details."

## CAR-02

**Asset ID:** CAR-02
**Date:** June 18 (Day 18 — enrollment push, final days)
**Platform:** Instagram + Facebook (FB-04)
**C/C/V:** Value / Conversion
**Track:** B
**Format:** 8-slide carousel
**Concept:** "What 6FIX is (and what it's not)"
**Frame:** Positioning carousel — clears objections and defines the program against what people assume fitness programs are.

---

### Cover (Slide 1) — Hook

**Headline:** What 6FIX is. And what it's not.

**Subtext:** (none)

**Design notes:** Charcoal background. Cormorant Garamond Italic headline, large. Orange accent. This cover should feel different from CAR-01 — slightly more editorial in tone.

---

### Slide 2 — Not a Challenge

**Headline:** It's not a 30-day challenge

**Body:** No countdown timer. No "complete 30 days and win a prize" mechanic.

Challenges work on novelty. They end on day 30. 6FIX is designed to outlast the novelty.

**Design notes:** Off-white. Clean, direct. Could add a subtle strikethrough on "challenge" as a design element.

---

### Slide 3 — Not Random Workouts

**Headline:** It's not a collection of sessions

**Body:** Each week connects to the next. The training load builds deliberately. Recovery is part of the plan, not an afterthought.

What you do in Week 3 is designed around what happened in Weeks 1 and 2.

**Design notes:** Off-white. Minimal. Let the words carry the slide.

---

### Slide 4 — Not a Quick Fix

**Headline:** It's not a detox or a reset

**Body:** No meal plan that resets at week 1 each time. No generic "eat clean, train hard" instruction.

Nutrition in 6FIX starts with what your schedule actually allows — then builds from there.

**Design notes:** Off-white. Could use a small "✗" symbol as a design element for the "not" framing — subtle, not heavy-handed.

---

### Slide 5 — What It Is

**Headline:** It's a coached system

**Body:** Structured training that progresses. Nutrition that fits a real schedule. Habit coaching built around the specific weeks where people usually stop. Weekly check-ins where the plan adjusts in real time.

Six weeks with a coach who's watching what the data shows — not just what you report.

**Design notes:** Charcoal background — visual shift to mark the "here's what it IS" turn. Off-white text. This is the pivot slide.

---

### Slide 6 — What Coaching Actually Means

**Headline:** What "weekly check-in" means in practice

**Body:** Not a form. Not a rating scale.

A review of what happened that week — what the data shows, what was hard, what needs to change. Then the plan updates. You don't get the same plan in week 4 that you got in week 1 if week 1 didn't go the way it was supposed to.

**Design notes:** Charcoal or off-white — either works here. Keep the body copy to 4 lines max.

---

### Slide 7 — What Six Weeks Builds

**Headline:** What happens in six weeks

**Body:** Week 1 builds a baseline. Weeks 2–4 build and test the habits. Weeks 5–6 are where the habits stop being decisions and start being defaults.

By the end of Phase 1, the structure is yours — not something you're borrowing from the program.

**Design notes:** Off-white. This is the outcomes slide — make it clean and specific. No vague language.

---

**Body:** 8 spots. Cohort 1 starts June 22.

Free onboarding call is June 19 — video conference, about 45 minutes. No commitment at that point.

DM me "6FIX."
