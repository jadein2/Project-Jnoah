## OVERVIEW

**Duration:** 6 Weeks
**Format:** 1-on-1 Coaching
**Platform:** Trainerize

## WHAT PHASE 2 IS

Phase 2 is individualized. Everything is personal — the nutrition targets, the routines, the exercise schedule, the habit stack. No group pacing. This is your system, built around your life.

**The 4 Pillars:**
1. Nutrition Architecture — personal protein targets, meal timing, food systems
2. Habit Installation — morning/evening routines, daily anchors, trigger management
3. Movement System — structured exercise + daily movement protocol
4. Accountability — weekly coaching reviews, daily tracking, personal adjustments

## PHASE 2 WEEKLY STRUCTURE

---

### WEEK 0 — ONBOARDING & SYSTEM AUDIT

**Theme:** From awareness to architecture.

**Coach Actions:**
- Conduct in-person onboarding session
- Review Phase 1 results (weight, waist, habit score, wins/gaps)
- Complete lifestyle audit (schedule, sleep, stress, eating patterns)
- Set personal protein target
- Build first draft of personal daily framework

**Client Deliverables:**
- Completed lifestyle audit form
- Personal protein target confirmed
- First-week meal structure drafted

- Trainerize set up and active
- Commitment video recorded (private)

**Key Message:**
"We're not starting over. We're building on what you already know."

---

### WEEK 1 — NUTRITION ARCHITECTURE

**Theme:** Build your personal food system.

**Focus:**
- Lock in protein targets per meal
- Identify 3 default meals (repeatable, practical, high-protein)
- Establish meal timing based on client schedule
- Remove 1 key friction point in eating
- Continue food logging — now with protein tracking

**Daily Nutrition Habits:**
- Hit daily protein target
- Log all meals in Trainerize
- Eat protein first at every meal
- Remove liquid sugar (continuing from Phase 1)

**Coach Action:**
- Review food logs twice this week
- Adjust default meals if protein is consistently low
- Flag any scheduling conflicts with meal timing

**Key Message:**
"Your food system should fit your life — not the other way around."

---

### WEEK 2 — MORNING & EVENING ANCHORS

**Theme:** Routines are the system.

**Focus:**
- Design a personalized morning routine (15–30 min)
- Design a personalized evening routine (10–20 min)
- Establish a consistent sleep window
- Add one stress management habit

**Morning Routine Template (Personalized):**
- Wake time locked
- Hydration first (500ml water)
- Light movement or walk
- Breakfast with protein anchor (25–40g)
- Food logged before leaving the house

**Evening Routine Template (Personalized):**
- Meal complete 2–3 hours before bed
- Wind-down signal (no screens, reading, stretching)
- Prep tomorrow's meals/clothes/schedule
- Lights out by consistent time

**Coach Action:**
- Review if routines are realistic for client's life
- Adjust based on Week 1 data
- Check sleep consistency (logged or self-reported)

**Key Message:**
"Your morning sets the standard. Your evening protects it."

---

### WEEK 3 — MOVEMENT SYSTEM

**Theme:** Movement becomes automatic.

**Focus:**
- Lock in 2x/week training schedule
- Set personalized daily step target
- Define active recovery days
- Add post-meal walks (10 min minimum)

**Training Protocol:**
- Minimum: 2 sessions/week (gym, home, or combination)
- Session length: 45–60 min
- Focus: Compound movements, progressive overload
- Recovery days: walking + mobility (not full rest)

**Step Targets by Level:**
- Starting point: 7,000 steps/day
- Intermediate: 9,000–10,000 steps/day
- Advanced: 11,000–12,000 steps/day
(Adjusted based on client's Phase 1 average)

**Coach Action:**
- Assign workouts in Trainerize
- Review exercise completion rate
- Confirm step target is realistic

**Key Message:**
"Consistency in movement beats intensity. Show up. Do the work."

---

### WEEK 4 — WEEKEND & SOCIAL STRUCTURE

**Theme:** Real life doesn't break the system.

**Focus:**
- Build a weekend nutrition plan (different from weekdays)
- Create an eating-out strategy (3 default restaurant choices)
- Define a buffer meal concept
- Manage stress-eating triggers with a decision script

**Weekend Rules (Client Builds Their Own):**
- Keep protein target — everything else is flexible
- One "free meal" is allowed — not a free day
- Log it anyway (no judgment, just data)
- Weekend workouts count — don't skip because it's Saturday

**Eating Out Framework:**
- Protein first: scan the menu for the highest-protein option
- Skip the bread/rice unless training that day
- Drink water or unsweetened drinks
- Portion: if unsure, use the hand method

**Trigger Management:**
- Identify top 2 eating triggers (stress, boredom, social pressure)
- Build a decision script for each: "When [trigger], I will [action] instead."

**Coach Action:**
- Review weekend logs specifically
- Identify patterns in weekend performance
- Adjust if client is consistently losing progress on weekends

**Key Message:**
"The plan doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to survive real life."

---

### WEEK 5 — REFINEMENT & HABIT STACKING

**Theme:** Systems compound.

**Focus:**
- Full system review: what's working, what isn't
- Stack 1–2 new micro-habits onto existing anchors
- Adjust nutrition targets if needed (based on 4-week data)
- Increase step target if hitting consistently

**Habit Stacking Examples:**
- After I finish breakfast → I log my food immediately
- After I brush my teeth at night → I prep tomorrow's protein
- After I put on my gym shoes → I do not check my phone until after the workout

**Nutrition Adjustment Triggers:**
- Weight stalled for 2+ weeks → reduce carbs or tighten portions
- Energy consistently low → increase carbs pre-training
- Hunger out of control → increase protein or eating frequency

**Coach Action:**
- Full mid-program review call or message
- Set Week 6 final goals
- Celebrate wins explicitly — document what changed

**Key Message:**
"You are no longer relying on motivation. You are running a system."

## NUTRITION TARGETS — PHASE 2

### Protein Target Formula
Goal body weight (lbs) × 0.8 = daily protein target in grams

**Examples:**
- Goal weight 130 lbs → 104g protein/day
- Goal weight 150 lbs → 120g protein/day
- Goal weight 170 lbs → 136g protein/day

### Meal Distribution
Divide daily target across 3 meals minimum:
- Breakfast: 25–35g
- Lunch: 30–40g
- Dinner: 30–40g
- Optional snack: 15–20g

### Carb Timing
- Before training: moderate carbs (rice, fruit, oats)
- After training: protein priority, moderate carbs
- Rest days: reduce carbs, keep protein constant

### Non-Negotiable Rules
- Protein at every meal — no exceptions
- Whole foods dominate — processed food is occasional
- No liquid sugar (continues from Phase 1)
- Water minimum: 2.5–3L per day

## HABIT SCORING SYSTEM — PHASE 2

### Daily Habits (Tracked in Trainerize)

| Habit | Points |
|-------|--------|
| Hit daily protein target | 1 |
| Logged all meals | 1 |
| Completed training session (if scheduled) | 1 |
| Hit daily step target | 1 |
| Completed morning routine | 1 |
| Completed evening routine | 1 |
| Hit water target | 1 |
| Hit sleep target (7+ hours) | 1 |

**Weekly max:** 56 points (8 habits × 7 days)
**Weekly score %:** Points earned ÷ Points possible × 100

### Score Benchmarks
- 85–100%: Outstanding — system is running
- 70–84%: Strong — minor adjustments needed
- 55–69%: Needs attention — coach reviews friction points
- Below 55%: Intervention — coach check-in call this week
