# 1. Executive Summary

Urban Strong Fitness has built a strong foundation as a coaching-driven fitness business. The business has clear strengths in coaching quality, client relationships, community culture, structured training, and beginner support. However, the current model still carries several limitations that may restrict growth, profitability, and scalability.

The main issue is not the lack of programs or coaching skill. The bigger issue is that the business has several offers, systems, and positioning angles that need to be organized into one stronger operating model.

Urban Strong Fitness currently operates between multiple identities:

- A coached group fitness gym
- A CrossFit-style functional fitness facility
- A personal training provider
- A nutrition and habit coaching service
- A beginner transformation program provider
- A potential hybrid/HYROX-style training space
- A possible corporate wellness provider
- A traditional gym access model opportunity

These can all become valuable parts of the business, but only if they are placed under one clear strategic model.

The next version of Urban Strong Fitness should position the business as a premium local coaching gym with multiple revenue streams built around one main promise:

> Urban Strong Fitness helps busy adults become leaner, stronger, healthier, and more consistent through structured training, coaching, nutrition guidance, and accountability.

The biggest opportunity is to move from a simple membership gym into a hybrid coaching and access model. This allows the business to serve more people, create a lower-cost access option, increase passive revenue potential, and still protect the strongest unique selling proposition of the brand: coaching.

# 2. Current Business Snapshot

## 2.1 Current Business Identity

Urban Strong Fitness is currently best understood as a coaching-based fitness business rather than a simple gym access facility.

The business offers group training, personal training, nutrition coaching, foundational onboarding, and transformation programs. The brand has an advantage because it is built around coaching, not just equipment access.

## 2.2 Current Strengths

| Area | Strength |
|---|---|
| Coaching | Strong coaching standards and movement education |
| Community | Loyal and supportive member culture |
| Programming | Structured functional fitness and strength training approach |
| Beginner Support | Foundation course and assessment-based onboarding |
| Client Results | Existing transformation stories and testimonials |
| Brand Trust | Local recognition and coach-led reputation |
| Service Range | Group classes, personal training, nutrition, workshops, and programs |
| Owner Expertise | Strong leadership from Coach Jap as head coach and operator |

## 2.3 Current Business Challenge

Urban Strong Fitness is not struggling because the product is weak. The business challenge is that the full value of the product may not be communicated, packaged, and sold clearly enough.

The market may still compare Urban Strong Fitness against cheaper gyms because the difference between gym access and coaching is not always clear to prospects.

This creates a positioning problem:

> If people see Urban Strong as gym access, the price feels expensive.  
> If people see Urban Strong as a coaching system, the price becomes easier to justify.

# 4. Weak Spots and Risk Areas

## 4.1 Positioning Weak Spot

Urban Strong Fitness has strong coaching value but may still be perceived as a regular gym by the public.

### Risk

People compare Urban Strong against cheaper gyms based only on monthly membership price.

### Recommended Fix

Strengthen the public position:

> We are not just gym access. We are a coached fitness system.

---

## 4.2 Offer Architecture Weak Spot

There are many offers, but the hierarchy is not yet fully simplified.

### Risk

Prospects may feel unclear about where to start or which program is right for them.

### Recommended Fix

Use one simple pathway:

**Assessment → Foundation → Core Membership → Upgrade Path**

---

## 4.3 Sales System Weak Spot

The sales process has strong pieces, but it needs tighter standardization.

### Risk

Leads may be lost due to inconsistent follow-up, unclear scripts, or weak objection handling.

### Recommended Fix

Create a defined sales pipeline:

1. New Lead
2. Contacted
3. Qualified
4. Assessment Booked
5. Assessment Completed
6. Offer Presented
7. Closed
8. Follow-Up / Nurture

---

## 4.4 Retention Weak Spot

Retention is supported by community and coaching relationships, but it needs stronger systems.

### Risk

Members who reduce attendance may quietly churn without early intervention.

### Recommended Fix

Create a member risk system:

| Attendance Pattern | Action |
|---|---|
| 7 days absent | Check-in message |
| 14 days absent | Coach follow-up |
| 21 days absent | Reactivation conversation |
| 30 days inactive | Save or exit strategy |

---

## 4.5 Financial Weak Spot

The business needs clearer unit economics per offer.

### Risk

Some services may look profitable but may consume too much coach time, admin work, or marketing cost.

### Recommended Fix

Track:

- Revenue per offer
- Profit per offer
- Coach cost per offer
- Average member lifetime value
- Churn rate
- Cost per lead
- Cost per acquisition
- Average revenue per member

---

## 4.6 Operations Weak Spot

Urban Strong has playbooks and SOPs, but the next requirement is management discipline.

### Risk

Good systems may exist but may not be executed consistently by staff and coaches.

### Recommended Fix

Create weekly scorecards for:

- Sales
- Lead follow-up
- Attendance
- Class quality
- Coach performance
- Member retention
- Revenue
- Admin compliance

# 8. Access-Only Model Opportunity

## 8.1 Traditional Access Model

The traditional gym model allows members to come in, use equipment, and train on their own.

This can create a lower-cost, more passive revenue stream for the business.

### Benefits

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Lower price entry | Attracts people who cannot afford full coaching membership |
| More market reach | Captures traditional gym users |
| Passive revenue potential | Less direct coaching required per member |
| Higher facility utilization | Gym can earn outside group class hours |
| Upsell potential | Access members can later upgrade to coaching |

### Limitations

| Limitation | Risk |
|---|---|
| Requires equipment investment | Business may need more machines, racks, dumbbells, and cardio tools |
| More facility wear and tear | Higher usage increases maintenance needs |
| Lower coaching control | Members may train with poor technique if unguided |
| Brand dilution | Business may look like a regular gym if not positioned properly |
| Space limitation | Current floor plan must support both classes and open gym |

---

# 9. Coached Access Model as the USP

The main opportunity is not just to offer gym access.

The stronger opportunity is to offer:

> Gym access with coaching support available.

This creates a new category between cheap access gyms and full coaching memberships.

## 9.1 Core Concept

Urban Strong Fitness can create a coach area that works like a lifeguard post.

The coach is not doing full personal training for every access member, but the coach is present to:

- Observe the training floor
- Guide basic safety
- Answer simple training questions
- Help members use equipment properly
- Correct risky movements when needed
- Recommend better training options
- Encourage upgrades to coaching when appropriate

## 9.2 Positioning Statement

> Train on your own, but never completely alone.

## 9.3 Stronger USP Version

> Most gyms give you equipment and leave you to figure it out. Urban Strong gives you access to the gym with coaches available to guide you when you need help.

This protects the coaching identity while allowing a more affordable membership layer.

# 12. Equipment and Investment Considerations

The access model requires more equipment than a pure class-based coaching model.

## 12.1 Possible Equipment Needs

- Additional dumbbells
- More benches
- More squat racks or half racks
- Cable machine
- Leg press or lower-body machine
- Cardio machines
- Storage upgrades
- Flooring improvements
- Mirrors or training zone markers
- Check-in and access control system

## 12.2 Investment Risk

Buying more equipment can increase the value of the facility, but it must be tied to clear revenue targets.

Before purchasing equipment, the business should calculate:

- Expected number of access members
- Price per access membership
- Monthly revenue target
- Equipment cost
- Payback period
- Added maintenance cost
- Space impact on group classes

# 13. Strategic Risks of Version 2

## 13.1 Brand Dilution

If not positioned properly, the access model may make Urban Strong look like a regular gym.

### Control

The access model must be branded as coached access, not cheap access.

---

## 13.2 Space Conflict

Open gym users may interfere with class operations.

### Control

Create time blocks and training zones.

---

## 13.3 Coach Overload

The floor coach may become distracted from primary coaching duties.

### Control

Define what floor support includes and does not include.

---

## 13.4 Poor Member Expectations

Access members may expect full personal training for a low price.

### Control

Create clear membership terms.

The coach can guide safety and basic use, but full programming and personal coaching require an upgrade.

---

# 14. Recommended Controls for Coached Access

## 14.1 Coach Floor Role

The coach-floor role should include:

- Basic equipment guidance
- Safety monitoring
- Basic movement corrections
- Short training suggestions
- New member orientation
- Upgrade recommendations

The coach-floor role should not include:

- Full personal training sessions
- Custom programming for every access member
- Nutrition coaching
- Long consultations during peak hours
- Full movement assessment without appointment

---

## 14.2 Suggested Access Rules

- Access members must complete basic gym orientation.
- Beginners must be screened before using complex equipment or barbell movements.
- Class areas may be restricted during class times.
- Coach guidance is limited to basic support unless upgraded.
- Members must follow equipment rules and safety standards.
- Program design is not included unless part of a higher-tier membership.

# 16. Strategic Recommendation

The recommended next model is not to replace the current model.

The better move is to build a layered model:

## Layer 1: Gym Access

Affordable, self-directed training with clear rules and orientation.

## Layer 2: Coached Access

Open gym access with coach-floor support and safety guidance.

## Layer 3: Group Coaching

Structured coached classes for members who want programming and community.

## Layer 4: Coaching Plus

Group training with nutrition, habit guidance, or progress review.

## Layer 5: Premium Coaching

Personal training, semi-private coaching, or transformation programs.

This allows Urban Strong Fitness to serve different types of clients without becoming a cheap gym.

# 21. Final Audit Conclusion

Urban Strong Fitness has a strong foundation and a clear opportunity to evolve.

The business does not need to abandon its current model. The better strategy is to organize the current coaching model and add a controlled access model that increases market reach and passive income potential.

The next version should be built around this idea:

> Urban Strong Fitness is not just a gym. It is a coaching-first fitness facility where members can choose the level of guidance they need.

This allows the business to serve:

- People who want affordable access
- People who want guidance but not full coaching
- People who want structured group training
- People who need personal training
- People who need nutrition and habit coaching
- People who want long-term transformation

The opportunity is to move from one main membership model into a layered fitness business model with stronger retention, clearer sales pathways, and more scalable revenue streams.
