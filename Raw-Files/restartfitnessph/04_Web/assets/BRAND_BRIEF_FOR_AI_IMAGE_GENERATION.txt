SECTION 1 — BRAND IDENTITY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BRAND NAME:
Restart Fitness PH

BUSINESS UNIT (current):
Urban Strong Fitness — a local gym and coaching studio operating under Restart Fitness PH.

BRAND MISSION:
"The Restart Method is a coaching system that helps busy adults turn effort into real
progress through structure, practical guidance, and accountability."

BRAND POSITIONING:
- Structured coaching for busy adults
- Not a generic gym. Not a hype brand.
- Professional, direct, empathetic, results-focused
- Anti-hype, anti-shortcut, anti-random workouts
- Target audience: busy professionals, working adults aged 25–45, people who have
  tried before and want a system that sticks

APPROVED HEADLINE COPY (use verbatim as text overlays when needed):
- "Stop guessing. Follow a system."
- "No hype. No shortcuts. Just a system that gets results."
- "No random WODs. No FADs."
- "We coach people who commit."
- "Consistency is coached through accountability."
- "The goal is not just results. The goal is results you can keep."

BRAND KEYWORDS (use to inform visual mood and subject matter):
Structure, System, Coaching, Accountability, Consistency, Clarity, Fundamentals,
Real-life fitness, Measurable progress, Guidance, Results that stick,
Practical nutrition, Long-term

  Gold — Authority / Results / Premium:             #C9A84C
  Gold Dark:                                        #8A7034
  Gold Light:                                       #E8C870

COLOR RULES FOR IMAGE GENERATION:
- Dark backgrounds (#1A1A18 / #242420) are dominant for hero, social, and coaching imagery
- Orange (#E06D22) appears only on ONE element per image — a word, a line, a shape
- Gold (#C9A84C) is used for results, testimonials, authority signals — never with orange on same image
- Off-white (#F5F5F0) is used for light editorial content — soft and clean, not bright white
- Never use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000)
- Never use gradients — flat, solid color fields only
- Never use more than 2 accent colors in a single image
- No neon, no vibrant rainbow palettes, no fluorescent tones

SECTION 3 — TYPOGRAPHY REFERENCE (FOR TEXT OVERLAY IMAGES)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DISPLAY / HEADLINE FONT:
  Name: Cormorant Garamond
  Style: Italic only (never upright)
  Weight: Light (300)
  Role: All headlines, campaign text, pull quotes, section titles
  Character: Elegant, editorial, premium serif

BODY / UI FONT:
  Name: DM Sans
  Weight: Light (300) for body | Medium (500) for labels and overlines
  Role: All body copy, overlines, button text, captions
  Character: Clean, modern, minimal sans-serif

PAIRING LOGIC:
  - Headline in Cormorant Garamond Italic (large, light weight)
  - Supporting copy in DM Sans Light
  - Labels/overlines in DM Sans 500, ALL CAPS, wide letter-spacing (0.15em)
  - One key word in the headline colored orange (#E06D22), rest in off-white or charcoal

TYPE SCALE (golden ratio φ = 1.618 from 16px base):
  Campaign Display:  68px — Cormorant Italic — hero and campaign images
  Page Headline:     42px — Cormorant Italic — section headers
  Section Title:     26px — Cormorant Italic — card titles, sub-sections
  Subhead:           20px — Cormorant Italic — intro text
  Body:              16px — DM Sans 300
  Label/Overline:    10px — DM Sans 500 — uppercase — tracking 0.15em

SECTION 4 — VISUAL STYLE DIRECTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OVERALL AESTHETIC:
  Minimalist editorial fitness — not gym-bro, not luxury spa.
  High contrast. Dark and purposeful. Clean negative space.
  Think: editorial magazine meets serious coaching studio.

MOOD WORDS:
  Disciplined, Grounded, Professional, Clean, Confident, Purposeful,
  Structured, Real, Modern, Premium without being flashy

WHAT THE IMAGES SHOULD FEEL LIKE:
  - A serious coaching environment — not flashy, not intimidating
  - Real people doing real work — not overly staged
  - Dark, moody lighting with strong contrast
  - Clean, uncluttered compositions
  - Purposeful use of space — wide negative space is good
  - Orange accents used sparingly — one per image maximum
  - Feels aspirational but accessible — not elite bodybuilding, not beginner spin class

COMPOSITION RULES:
  - Strong vertical or horizontal grid alignment
  - Subject centered or following rule of thirds
  - Leave clear space for text overlays (especially top and bottom thirds)
  - Avoid center-heavy compositions with no breathing room
  - Use asymmetry to create editorial tension


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5 — WHAT TO INCLUDE IN IMAGES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

APPROVED SUBJECT MATTER:
  - Adults (25–45) doing compound functional movements: squats, deadlifts, presses, rows
  - Coach-client interaction: coaching form, discussing a program, monitoring a session
  - Real gym environment: barbells, racks, platforms — clean and functional, not overcrowded
  - Focused individuals mid-set — concentration, not performance for camera
  - Group training: structured, disciplined — not chaotic boot camp style
  - Individual working alone in a well-lit gym space
  - Simple nutrition context: meal prep, real food — not supplements or diet culture imagery
  - Movement: walking, stretching post-session, warm-ups — everyday athleticism
  - Planning or coaching session: clipboard, whiteboard, conversation between coach and client
  - Dark studio atmosphere: spotlit subjects against dark backgrounds
  - Overhead or clean flat-lay: training equipment on neutral surface (mat, platform)

APPROVED ENVIRONMENTS:
  - Functional training gym — clean, minimal equipment
  - Outdoor training if well-lit and purposeful
  - Studio-style coaching space (dark, controlled lighting)
  - Urban/city-adjacent training settings
  - Neutral indoor spaces (homes, offices) for nutrition/lifestyle content


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 6 — WHAT TO EXCLUDE FROM IMAGES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DO NOT INCLUDE:
  - Shirtless flexing or physique-first poses
  - Before/after body comparison imagery
  - Extreme leanness, bodybuilder aesthetics
  - Overly vibrant or saturated color grading
  - Protein shake / supplement branding
  - Generic stock photo gym aesthetics (smiling perfectly into camera mid-exercise)
  - Crowded, chaotic, unstructured gym environments
  - Hype-style imagery (explosive action, screaming, intense aggression)
  - Children or minors
  - Heavy filters or stylized photo treatments (LUTs, high-grain vintage, overblown highlights)
  - Busy background clutter or visual noise
  - Logos of other brands or gyms visible in scene
  - Any text in fonts other than Cormorant Garamond or DM Sans (for text overlay images)

SECTION 10 — BRAND VOICE REFERENCE (FOR CAPTION / TEXT OVERLAY COPY)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TONE: Direct. Confident. Empathetic. Uncompromising.

WRITE LIKE:
  "Here's the process. Let's execute."
  "Your schedule is real. So is our program."
  "Effort without structure is just fatigue."

DO NOT WRITE LIKE:
  "Transform your body!" (hype)
  "Start your fitness journey!" (generic)
  "Feel the burn!" (motivational filler)
  "Restart your life!" (restart cannot be used as a verb)
  "Tailored just for you" (use "structured for you" instead)
  "Last chance to sign up!" (false urgency)

BANNED PHRASE: "the last first step" — permanently prohibited

CTA HIERARCHY:
  1. Book a Discovery Call       (website — primary)
  2. DM "RESTART"                (Instagram / Facebook)
  3. Learn the System            (secondary, ghost button)
  4. Explore Urban Strong        (secondary, ghost button)
