## Idea #1 — Instagram

- **Format:** Carousel (5 slides)
- **Hook / Headline:** "5 exercises you're probably doing wrong (and the easy fix)"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Squat depth and knee tracking — most people stop too high or let knees cave in
  - Plank form — hips sagging or piking up defeats the purpose
  - Deadlift hinge — it's a hip hinge, not a back bend

## Idea #6 — Both (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Format:** Carousel / educational post (myth-busting)
- **Hook / Headline:** "3 fitness myths that are keeping you stuck (backed by science)"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth 1: "You need to feel sore to know it was a good workout" — soreness means unfamiliar stimulus, not progress
  - Myth 2: "Lifting heavy will make you bulky" — building significant muscle mass requires very specific, sustained effort; strength training leans you out
  - Myth 3: "You have to cut carbs to lose fat" — carbs are your body's preferred fuel source; it's overall calorie balance and food quality that matter
  - Each myth gets a "The Truth" slide with a simple, science-based correction
- **Caption Starter:** "If you've believed any of these, you're not alone — they're everywhere. But they're also holding a lot of people back from real progress. Let's clear them up."
- **CTA:** "Share this with someone who needs to hear myth #2. And save it so you remember the truth next time one of these pops up in your feed."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, science-backed, education, strength training, fat loss
