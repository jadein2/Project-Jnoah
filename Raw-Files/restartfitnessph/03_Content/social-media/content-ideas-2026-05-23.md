### Idea #2 — Instagram

- **Format:** Reel (30–45 sec, coach-to-camera + demo clips)
- **Hook / Headline:** "You're doing push-ups wrong — and it's why your shoulders hurt."
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Most people flare their elbows out at 90 degrees — that loads the shoulder joint, not the chest
  - Cue: tuck elbows to about 45 degrees, think "arrow shape" not "T shape"
  - Hands should be stacked under your shoulders, not out in front
  - Show the wrong form vs. correct form side by side
  - Even 5 good reps beat 20 sloppy ones — quality builds strength, not volume alone
- **Caption Starter:** If push-ups hurt your shoulders more than your chest, this fix takes 10 seconds to learn and changes everything.
- **CTA:** Try 10 push-ups with this form today and drop a comment on how it felt.
- **Hashtag Themes:** push-up form, exercise tips, beginner workout, injury prevention, movement quality

### Idea #6 — Instagram

- **Format:** Carousel (4 slides — myth vs. fact format)
- **Hook / Headline:** "4 fitness myths that are keeping you stuck"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth 1: "You need to feel sore after every workout for it to count." → Fact: Soreness means novelty, not progress. Consistent effort without crippling DOMS is the goal.
  - Myth 2: "Carbs after 6pm make you fat." → Fact: Total intake matters more than timing. Your body doesn't have a carb curfew.
  - Myth 3: "Lifting heavy will make women bulky." → Fact: It takes years of specific training and surplus calories to build significant mass. Strength training builds shape, not bulk.
  - Myth 4: "More sweat = more fat burned." → Fact: Sweat is temperature regulation, not a fat-loss meter. A tough strength session might not drench you but still burn more over 24 hours.
- **Caption Starter:** If you still believe any of these, no shame — the fitness industry has been recycling bad information for decades. Let's clear it up.
- **CTA:** Which myth did you used to believe? Be honest — I believed #2 for years.
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, fact check, science-based fitness, education, coaching
