### Facebook Version (longer):

Hustle culture had us convinced that slowing down means giving up. That rest is for the uncommitted. That if you're tired, you just need to push harder.

I've worked with enough people to know that this mindset causes more harm than any missed workout ever could.

Here are 5 signs your body is asking you to slow down — not quit:

**1. You're exhausted even after a full night's sleep.**
This is one of the clearest signs of overtraining. When quality rest stops feeling restorative, your nervous system is overwhelmed. More training won't fix this — recovery will.

**2. Your mood is off.**
Irritability, anxiety, emotional flatness — these aren't personality issues. They're hormonal signals. Elevated cortisol from chronic overtraining directly affects your brain. Your body is under stress even when you're "just exercising."

**3. Your progress has completely stalled.**
A plateau after consistent hard training is rarely a sign to add more. It's usually a sign your body hasn't had enough time to adapt and rebuild. A deload week often unlocks more progress than another hard month.

**4. Your cravings are spiking.**
Intense cravings for sugar or carbs aren't a willpower failure. They're your nervous system calling for fast fuel during a period of chronic physical stress. Honor that signal — eat enough, eat well.

**5. You're dreading workouts instead of looking forward to them.**
There's a big difference between a rest day and dreading every session. When movement stops feeling like something you want to do and starts feeling like something you have to survive — that's your sign.

Slowing down is not the same as stopping.
The athletes and clients who go the distance are the ones who learn to *listen* — and respond.
