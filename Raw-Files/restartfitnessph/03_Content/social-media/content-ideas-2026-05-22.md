### Idea #2 — Instagram

- **Format:** Reel (30–45 sec, coach-to-camera + B-roll of movement demos)
- **Hook / Headline:** "The warm-up you're skipping is costing you gains."
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Cold muscles don't fire at full capacity — you're literally leaving strength on the table
  - A 5-minute dynamic warm-up primes your nervous system, not just your muscles
  - Quick protocol: hip circles, arm swings, bodyweight squats, inchworms, lateral lunges
  - Injury prevention is a gain — being sidelined for weeks sets you back more than 5 minutes ever will
  - Warm-ups also shift your mental state from "scrolling mode" to "training mode"
- **Caption Starter:** You wouldn't start a car in freezing weather and floor it immediately. Your body works the same way.
- **CTA:** Try this 5-minute warm-up before your next session and tell me it doesn't feel different.
- **Hashtag Themes:** warm-up routine, injury prevention, workout tips, beginner fitness, training smarter

### Idea #5 — Both (Instagram & Facebook)

- **Format:** Single-image infographic with bold text
- **Hook / Headline:** "What your post-workout meal actually needs (it's simpler than you think)."
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Protein within 1–2 hours (not a magic 30-minute window — that's mostly myth)
  - Carbs to replenish glycogen, especially after intense sessions
  - Don't overthink it: chicken and rice, eggs and toast, a protein shake and a banana all work
  - Hydration matters more than most people realize post-training
  - Stop spending 20 minutes choosing the "perfect" meal and just eat something solid
- **Caption Starter:** You just crushed a workout. Now what? The internet says 47 different things. Here's what actually matters.
- **CTA:** Save this so you stop overthinking your post-workout meal.
- **Hashtag Themes:** post-workout nutrition, simple nutrition, fitness tips, meal ideas, coaching

### Idea #7 — Both (Instagram & Facebook)

- **Format:** Carousel (5 slides — myth vs. fact format)
- **Hook / Headline:** "5 fitness 'rules' that are actually holding you back."
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth: You need to feel sore to know it worked → Fact: Soreness is not a reliable indicator of a good session
  - Myth: Carbs after 6pm make you gain fat → Fact: Total daily intake matters far more than timing
  - Myth: More sweat = more fat burned → Fact: Sweat is your body cooling down, not fat leaving
  - Myth: Women who lift heavy will get bulky → Fact: Building visible muscle takes years of dedicated effort
  - Myth: You have to eat clean 100% of the time → Fact: Rigid rules create binge-restrict cycles
- **Caption Starter:** If you're still following these "rules," they might be the reason you're stuck. Let's clear it up.
- **CTA:** Which myth did you believe the longest? Drop the number in the comments.
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, myth busting, evidence-based fitness, nutrition facts, coaching education
