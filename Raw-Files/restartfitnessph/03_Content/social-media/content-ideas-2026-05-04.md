### Idea #1 — Instagram

- **Format:** Carousel (5–7 slides)
- **Hook / Headline:** "5 exercises you're probably doing wrong (and how to fix them)"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Squat depth and knee tracking — most beginners go too shallow or let knees cave in
  - Plank form — hips sagging or piking up defeats the purpose
  - Lunges — front knee shooting past the toe and losing balance
  - Push-ups — flaring elbows out at 90° puts unnecessary stress on shoulders
  - Deadlift hinge — rounding the lower back instead of hinging at the hips
- **Caption Starter:** "You're showing up and putting in the work — that already makes you ahead of most people. But if these 5 moves feel awkward or painful, a small form fix could change everything."
- **CTA:** "Save this so you can check your form next session. Tag your gym buddy who needs to see slide 4."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness tips, exercise form, beginner fitness, workout coaching, gym mistakes

### Idea #6 — Both (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Format:** Carousel (educational — myth-busting)
- **Hook / Headline:** "3 fitness 'rules' that are actually holding you back"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth 1: "You need to work out every day to see results" — rest days are when your muscles actually grow and recover
  - Myth 2: "Lifting weights will make you bulky" — especially misleading for women; strength training builds lean, toned muscle
  - Myth 3: "No pain, no gain" — pain is your body's warning signal, not a badge of honor; soreness is okay, sharp pain is not
  - The truth: smart, consistent training with proper recovery beats grinding yourself into the ground
- **Caption Starter:** "If you've ever felt guilty for taking a rest day or avoided weights because you didn't want to 'get too big' — this one's for you. Let's unlearn the stuff that's actually slowing you down."
- **CTA:** "Save this and send it to someone who still believes myth #2. Let's spread facts, not fear."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, science-backed fitness, strength training, workout education, wellness facts
