## Full Caption

When I started coaching, I thought my job was to write the perfect workout plan. The right sets, the right reps, the right progression — that was supposed to be the thing that changed people's lives.

It took me a while to realize that the real work happens between the sets.

In the conversations after a tough session. In the mindset shifts that happen when someone finally stops saying "I can't" and starts asking "how do I?" In the small wins nobody else sees — showing up on a bad day, choosing water over soda, going to bed 30 minutes earlier.

Transformation isn't always physical. The biggest changes I've seen in my clients? It's in how they talk to themselves. How they carry themselves. How they stop waiting for the "right time" and just begin.

If you're a coach — or thinking about becoming one — here's what I wish someone told me on day one:

→ Your clients don't need a perfect plan. They need someone who believes in them when they don't believe in themselves.
→ Progress isn't linear, and that's okay. Every plateau is a lesson, not a dead end.
→ The best program in the world is the one your client will actually follow.

This work is bigger than fitness. It always has been.
