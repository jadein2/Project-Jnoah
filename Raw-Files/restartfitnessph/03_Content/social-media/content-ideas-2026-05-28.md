### Idea #1 — Instagram

- **Format:** Carousel (5 slides)
- **Hook / Headline:** "5 exercises you're doing wrong — and the easy fix for each"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Slide 1: Hook slide with bold text
  - Slide 2: Squats — knees caving in. Fix: push knees out over toes, engage glutes
  - Slide 3: Planks — hips sagging. Fix: squeeze glutes, tuck pelvis slightly
  - Slide 4: Lunges — front knee shooting past toes. Fix: step further out, sit back into the heel
  - Slide 5: Push-ups — flared elbows. Fix: tuck elbows at 45 degrees, not 90
  - Final slide: CTA to save + follow for more
- **Caption Starter:** "You're putting in the work — let's make sure your body is actually getting the benefit. Here are 5 common form mistakes I see every single week (and the quick fix for each)."
- **CTA:** "Save this for your next gym session and tag your workout buddy who needs to see slide 3."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness tips, exercise form, beginner fitness, workout mistakes, coaching

- **CTA:** "Double tap if you've ever forced yourself to start and ended up glad you did."
- **Hashtag Themes:** mindset, motivation, discipline, personal growth, consistency

### Idea #5 — Both (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Format:** Carousel (Myth-Busting — 4 slides)
- **Hook / Headline:** "3 fitness myths that are holding you back in 2026"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth 1: "You need to work out every day to see results." Truth: Rest days are when your body actually builds muscle
  - Myth 2: "Carbs make you gain weight." Truth: Carbs are your body's primary fuel source — it's total calories that matter
  - Myth 3: "Soreness = a good workout." Truth: Soreness is about novelty, not effectiveness
  - Final slide: "Stop chasing pain, start chasing progress."
- **Caption Starter:** "Still believe any of these? You're not alone — and it's not your fault. The fitness industry loves to overcomplicate things. Let me simplify it for you."
- **CTA:** "Share this with someone who needs to hear myth #2. And save it for the next time someone gives you bad gym advice."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, education, science-backed fitness, wellness, beginner tips

### Idea #7 — Both (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Format:** Single Image / Feed Post (Educational)
- **Hook / Headline:** "The 2-minute rule that changed how I build habits"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Introduce the 2-minute rule: scale any habit down to something that takes 2 minutes or less
  - "Go to the gym" becomes "put on your gym shoes"
  - "Eat healthy" becomes "add one vegetable to your plate"
  - The goal isn't the 2 minutes — it's becoming the type of person who shows up
  - Small wins compound into life-changing consistency
- **Caption Starter:** "If you keep falling off your routine, you're not lazy — your goals are just too big to start with. Try the 2-minute rule instead. Here's how it works."
- **CTA:** "Save this and try it tomorrow morning. Then come back and tell me how it went."
- **Hashtag Themes:** habits, personal growth, consistency, mindset, life coaching
