### SLIDE 2 — Myth #1
**Label:** MYTH #1

**The Myth:**
> "You need to feel sore to know your workout worked."

**The Truth:**
Soreness (DOMS) is caused by microscopic muscle damage — not growth. You can have an incredibly effective workout with zero soreness the next day. As your body adapts and gets stronger, soreness actually decreases. That's a good thing.

**Bottom line:** Soreness = novelty. Absence of soreness ≠ wasted effort.

**Design notes:** "MYTH" in red or strikethrough. "TRUTH" in brand green or gold. Keep text tight — one punchy fact per slide.

---

### SLIDE 3 — Myth #2
**Label:** MYTH #2

**The Myth:**
> "Cardio is the best way to lose fat."

**The Truth:**
Cardio burns calories during the workout. Strength training builds muscle — and muscle burns more calories at rest, 24/7. For sustainable fat loss, resistance training + a mild caloric deficit consistently beats endless treadmill sessions. Cardio has its place, but it's not the whole answer.

**Bottom line:** Lift to burn. Cardio to support. Both have value — context is everything.

---

### SLIDE 4 — Myth #3
**Label:** MYTH #3

**The Myth:**
> "Lifting weights will make women bulky."

**The Truth:**
Building significant muscle mass requires years of progressive training, a caloric surplus, and testosterone levels that most women simply don't have. What lifting actually does for women: increases metabolism, improves bone density, creates a toned and defined physique, and builds real functional strength.

**Bottom line:** You won't accidentally get bulky. You'll intentionally get strong.

---

### SLIDE 5 — Myth #4
**Label:** MYTH #4

**The Myth:**
> "You have to cut carbs to lose weight."

**The Truth:**
Carbohydrates are your body's preferred fuel source — especially for the brain and muscles during exercise. Weight loss comes from a caloric deficit, not from eliminating any single macronutrient. Cutting carbs works for some people because it reduces overall intake — not because carbs are inherently bad.

**Bottom line:** Carbs aren't the enemy. Excess calories are. Eat the rice. Train well.

---

### SLIDE 6 — Myth #5
**Label:** MYTH #5

**The Myth:**
> "No pain, no gain."

**The Truth:**
There's a difference between the healthy discomfort of pushing your limits and the warning signal of actual pain. Ignoring pain leads to injury — and injury leads to weeks or months off. Real progress is built sustainably, not through suffering. Listen to your body. It's always communicating.

**Bottom line:** Train hard. Train smart. Pain is not a badge of honor — it's a signal.
