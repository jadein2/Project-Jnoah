## Idea #1 — Instagram

- **Format:** Carousel (5 slides)
- **Hook / Headline:** "5 exercises you're doing wrong (and how to fix them TODAY)"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Squats: knees caving in — cue "spread the floor with your feet"
  - Planks: hips sagging or piking — focus on a straight line from head to heels
  - Lunges: front knee shooting past toes — step further out and sit back
  - Push-ups: flaring elbows at 90° — tuck elbows closer to 45°
  - Deadlifts: rounding the lower back — hinge at the hips, chest proud
- **Caption Starter:** "You don't need to do MORE exercises — you need to do them BETTER. Here are 5 common form mistakes I see every single week (slide through to fix yours)."
- **CTA:** "Save this so you can check your form next workout. Tag your gym buddy who needs this."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness tips, exercise form, workout mistakes, beginner fitness, coaching

## Idea #6 — Both (Instagram + Facebook)

- **Format:** Carousel / Educational post (myth-busting)
- **Hook / Headline:** "3 fitness myths that are STILL holding you back in 2026"
- **Key Points / Talking Points:**
  - Myth 1: "Lifting heavy will make you bulky" — Truth: building significant muscle mass takes years of dedicated surplus eating and training; strength training actually creates a lean, toned look
  - Myth 2: "You have to work out every day to see results" — Truth: rest days are when your muscles actually repair and grow; 3–4 quality sessions per week beats 7 burned-out ones
  - Myth 3: "Carbs are the enemy" — Truth: carbs are your body's preferred fuel source, especially for training; choose whole grains, fruits, and starchy vegetables
  - Bonus: the best program is the one you can stick to consistently
- **Caption Starter:** "I hear these EVERY week from new clients. And honestly? These myths are the reason so many people feel stuck. Let's clear them up for good."
- **CTA:** "Which myth surprised you the most? Comment below and share this with someone who still believes #2."
- **Hashtag Themes:** fitness myths, science-backed fitness, education, workout facts, wellness
