## INSTAGRAM CAPTION

It's not a new exercise. It's not a supplement.

The single habit that moved the needle most for my clients this year was this: writing one sentence after every workout about how they felt.

That's it. One sentence.

"Felt strong today — sleep was better last night."
"Low energy but still showed up. That matters."
"Hit a new PR on squats. Mood was great all day."

Here's why it works 👇

When you start paying attention to how you feel — not just what you lift or how you look — something shifts. You start noticing patterns. You see what fuels you and what drains you. You catch yourself before you spiral.

Self-awareness builds consistency.
Consistency builds results.

It takes 30 seconds. You don't need a fancy journal. Notes app. Voice memo. Scribble on your hand if you have to.

The point isn't the format. The point is that you pause, reflect, and give yourself credit for showing up.

Try it for 7 days and tell me what you notice.
