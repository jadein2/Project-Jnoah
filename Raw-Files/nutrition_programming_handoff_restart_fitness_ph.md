# Nutrition Programming Handoff — Restart Fitness PH / 6Fix

## Purpose

This file is the working nutrition programming snapshot for Coach Jap’s client meal-prep system.

Use this as the reference for creating beginner-friendly, client-ready nutrition plans that are practical, structured, high-protein, whole-food focused, and built around sustainable fat loss, recomposition, or performance support.

The approved output style is the same structure used for Aira Quito’s final plan:

1. Client Summary
2. Daily Targets
3. Per-Meal Macro Targets
4. 7-Day Course Meals
5. Recipes with Preparation + Cooking Instructions
6. 7-Day Meal Prep Plan
7. Grocery List with Total Weights/Pieces
8. Coaching Rules
9. Final PDF Copy with QA Check

# 1. Nutrition Programming Philosophy

## Core Coaching Principle

The client does not need to eat the same 4 meals every day.

Meal prep is used to reduce decision fatigue, improve consistency, and prevent unintended overeating. The goal is to batch-cook useful food components, then rotate them across meals.

## Main Priorities

1. Protein at every meal.
2. Whole foods first.
3. Vegetables daily.
4. Rice/starch controlled and matched to training demand.
5. Safe fats only.
6. Minimize sugar, sweet drinks, fried oils, and highly processed food.
7. Build repeatable habits before adding complexity.

## Simple Client Rule

Protein first. Vegetables every meal. Rice measured. Drinks controlled.

# 2. Nutrition Frameworks Used

## A. MetFix Practical Diet Protocol

Food priority pyramid:

1. Meat + Vegetables
2. Nuts + Seeds
3. Some Starch
4. Little Fruit

Avoid or minimize:

- Sugar
- Seed oils
- Highly processed foods
- Sugar-sweetened drinks

## B. Lifestyle Medicine Plate Principle

Use whole foods as the foundation.

Daily emphasis:

- More fruits and vegetables
- Adequate protein
- Controlled whole-food starch
- Limit added sugar, refined grains, highly processed foods, and sugar-sweetened beverages

## C. Macro Education

Carbs:
- Carbs convert to glucose.
- Primary role: energy.
- Training days may need more carbs.

Protein:
- Protein converts to amino acids.
- Primary role: structure, repair, muscle preservation.
- Contains essential amino acids.
- This is the main behavior target for beginners.

Fats:
- Fats convert to fatty acids.
- Primary role: hormones, energy, structure.
- Use safe fats and control portions.

# 4. Calculation Rules

## BMR Formula

Use Mifflin-St Jeor.

Female:
BMR = 10 x weight kg + 6.25 x height cm - 5 x age - 161

Male:
BMR = 10 x weight kg + 6.25 x height cm - 5 x age + 5

## Activity Multipliers

Use these unless client data suggests otherwise:

- Sedentary office: 1.20
- Light active: 1.35–1.45
- Moderate active: 1.50–1.60
- Very active: 1.70–1.80

## Deficit Rules

Fat loss:
- Default deficit: 10%
- Conservative deficit: 5% if stress, sleep, adherence, or training recovery is a concern

Maintenance/Recomp:
- 5% deficit or maintenance calories depending on goal and current adherence

Performance:
- Avoid aggressive deficits unless requested

# 5. Macro Programming Rules

## Default Beginner Fat Loss Split

Use a high-protein but sustainable split.

Suggested starting range:
- Protein: 30–40%
- Carbs: 35–45%
- Fats: 20–30%

## Protein-Forward Plan

Use this when the client has high body weight, fat loss goal, and training:

- Protein: 40%
- Carbs: 35%
- Fats: 25%

Example from Aira Quito:
- Target calories: 2,564 kcal/day
- Protein: 256g
- Carbs: 224g
- Fats: 71g

## Low-Carb / Keto-Leaning Special Plan

Use only when specifically requested.

Ratio:
- Carbs: 10%
- Protein: 50%
- Fats: 40%

Important:
- This is not the default.
- Apply only with clear coaching explanation.
- Keep it practical and not extreme.

## Protein Distribution

Distribute protein across all meals and snacks.

For 3 meals + 2 snacks:
- Breakfast: moderate-high protein
- Snack 1: protein smoothie or whey-based
- Lunch: highest protein and main fuel meal
- Snack 2: protein-based
- Dinner: salad + protein, lower carb if needed

# 6. Portion Rules

## Protein

Use cooked weights unless specified.

Common coaching guide:
- 1 palm cooked lean meat/fish = about 25–30g protein
- 2 palms cooked lean meat/fish = about 50–60g protein

## Rice

Use cooked weight or cooked cups.

Default breakfast rice cap:
- 120–150g cooked rice

Lunch:
- Higher rice portion allowed if training or high energy demand

Dinner:
- Often lower carb, especially if salad + protein structure is used

## Vegetables

Use cups for client ease.

Default:
- Lunch salad: 2 cups
- Dinner salad: 3–4 cups
- Cooked vegetables: 1–2 cups per meal

## Fats

Use only safe fats.

Approved fat sources:
- Olive oil
- Avocado
- Nuts
- Seeds
- Small amount of butter/ghee if appropriate
- Coconut oil in small amounts

Avoid:
- Seed oils when possible
- Deep-fried oils
- Unmeasured sauces
- Sweet dressings

# 7. Protein Cheat Sheet

Approximate 50g protein portions:

- Chicken breast cooked: 180–200g
- Fish cooked: 220–260g
- Shrimp cooked: 220–250g
- Lean beef cooked: 200–230g
- Lean pork cooked: 220–250g
- Canned tuna drained: about 2 small cans
- Egg whites: about 2 cups / 500ml
- Whole eggs: about 8 large eggs
- Whey protein: about 2 scoops depending on brand

Approximate 25g protein portions:

- Chicken breast cooked: 90–100g
- Fish cooked: 110–130g
- Lean pork cooked: 110–130g
- Lean beef cooked: 100–120g
- Shrimp cooked: 110–130g
- Egg whites: about 1 cup / 250ml
- Whole eggs: about 4 large eggs
- Whey protein: about 1 scoop depending on brand

# 10. Quality Control Checklist

Run this every time before final output or PDF.

## Nutrition Math

- TDEE calculation is reasonable.
- Deficit is correctly applied.
- Macro grams match target calories.
- Per-meal macros roughly add up to daily macros.
- Calories are not excessively low for training demand.

## Portion Accuracy

- Rice portions match rules.
- Breakfast rice cap is respected if provided.
- Snacks match the snack count.
- Salad/vegetable rules are followed.
- Protein portions are realistic.
- Processed foods are limited if used.

## Grocery Accuracy

- Grocery list matches the meal plan.
- Rice totals are not inflated.
- Protein totals are realistic for 7 days.
- Vegetables and fruits are listed in total weights or pieces.
- Condiments are included only when useful.

## Formatting

- Tables do not overlap.
- Text wraps properly.
- No visible HTML tags.
- No broken symbols.
- No cutoff table cells.
- Preview images are checked before final PDF.

## Client-Ready Language

Avoid:
- “AI-generated”
- “As an AI”
- “Here is the generated plan”
- “Let me know if you want”
- Excessive caveats
- Overly technical explanations

Use:
- Direct coaching language
- Clear portions
- Practical rules
- Professional dietitian/coach tone

# 12. Current Approved Workflow

Every client meal prep plan follows this sequence:

1. Ask intake questionnaire.
2. Calculate TDEE and calorie target.
3. Create daily macros.
4. Create per-meal macro targets.
5. Build 5-day or 7-day course meals.
6. Upgrade client go-to meals into whole-food versions.
7. Include recipes with prep/cooking instructions.
8. Build batch-cook meal prep system.
9. Create grocery list with totals.
10. Run QA diagnostics.
11. Generate final PDF.
12. Provide PDF + preview links.
