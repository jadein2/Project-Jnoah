## Highlights for Review

**Top T1/T2 finding:** Fitness & nutrition landing pages convert at ~13.2% on average [T2]. The single biggest structural lever is one page = one primary action — pages that try to sell everything sell nothing. Proof must appear early (in or right under the hero), not buried at the bottom.

**Top conversion-model signal:** The application-first model (a short qualifier form before the calendar opens) converts at 30–55% application-to-purchase, while cold "just book a call" discovery has dropped below 5% across most coaching niches [T2]. This directly affects how "Book a Discovery Call" should be built — gate it with a 2–4 field qualifier, don't drop people straight onto a raw calendar.

**Biggest competitive gap (web):** PH fitness sites are aesthetics/motivation-led with cluttered layouts and buried offers. A minimalist-editorial, system-led page where the offer and proof are findable in under 10 seconds is essentially unclaimed in the PH coaching web space — and it's exactly what the locked brand visual system ("Structure is the design") already mandates.

**Content seed worth acting on:** A 4-section homepage mapped to the proven persuasion arc — Curiosity (Hook) → Empathy (Problem→System) → Safety + Desire (Proof + Offer path) → Action (Lead capture). Every section headline is a reusable social hook. This closes Jap's "content must complement social" requirement structurally, not just stylistically.

**Weak signals (set aside):** The 13.2% fitness-LP average is a single aggregate figure — directional, not a target to promise. Specific competitor conversion claims (e.g., "97% of clients") are marketing claims, not verified data — do not imitate the number, only the structural confidence.

## Layer 2 — Hard Evidence [COMPLETE]

### 2A — Conversion benchmarks & the one-action rule
[T2 — Moosend, ConvertFlow, CXL, Apexure, 2026]
- Fitness & nutrition landing pages average ~13.2% conversion. Directional aggregate — use as a sanity band, not a promised figure.
- A page converts best when it sells **one** primary action. "Trying to sell everything on one page usually sells nothing." One CTA per page (homepage may carry one primary + one transitional).
- The visitor should see headline + value proposition + CTA **without scrolling** — especially on mobile. No oversized hero image that pushes the CTA below the fold.

### 2B — The persuasion sequence (section order)
[T2 — CXL, involve.me, KlientBoost, Debutify; corroborated across multiple sources]
Recommended high-converting order: **Hero → Problem → Solution → Social proof → CTA** (FAQ/footer after).
Underlying emotional arc every converting page runs:
1. **Curiosity** — the hook captures attention
2. **Empathy** — the copy mirrors their struggle ("you understand their pain better than they can describe it")
3. **Safety** — authority + proof remove doubt
4. **Desire** — the offer creates aspiration
5. **Action** — one clear next step
Key rule: **show proof early, not only at the bottom.**

### 2C — The application-first conversion model
[T2 — Leadpages, Heyflow coaching funnel data, 2026]
- Cold "book a call" discovery conversion has dropped **below 5%** across most coaching niches.
- The **application-first model** (short qualifier before the calendar opens) converts **30–55%** application-to-purchase.
- Pre-screening intake (goal, current situation, timeline — and budget where appropriate) qualifies the prospect *before* they see availability, lifting both show-rate and close-rate.
- Conditional logic: high-readiness → prompt to book the call; lower-readiness → route to a lead magnet / nurture instead.

### 2D — Form friction & booking flow
[T2 — Rework, OnceHub, 2026]
- Each additional required form field reduces completion by **3–5%**. Minimize fields ruthlessly.
- An optimal booking flow runs **60–90 seconds** from start to confirmation; every extra step sheds a percentage of bookers.
- Static "contact us" forms that ask a lot and offer nothing back convert poorly and attract poor-fit leads.

### 2E — Speed & mobile (hard constraints)
[T2 — multiple coaching-funnel + fitness-web sources, 2026]
- The majority of fitness/coaching web traffic is **mobile**. Funnel should load in **under 2 seconds**, target page-speed score **>90**, or impatient prospects leave before answering anything.
- Social proof should land within the **first ~8 seconds** of attention.

## Layer 3 — Pattern Data [COMPLETE]

### 3A — What's winning on coaching/fitness pages (2026)
[T2 — Hostinger, mypersonaltrainerwebsite, Elementor, 99designs, 2026]
- **Niche specificity wins.** Trainers who clearly target a specific person consistently outperform generalists in both search and conversion. "Online personal training for [specific person]" beats "Welcome to my website."
- **Human copy wins.** The standout 2026 sites read like a person wrote them — not templated marketing voice. (Direct alignment with the anti-ai-copywriter skill.)
- **Offer findable in <10 seconds.** If a visitor can't find what you offer, for whom, and how to enquire within ten seconds, they leave.
- **Specific proof beats generic.** The more specific the result (real name, real number, real timeframe), the more credible. Generic "transformation" claims are discounted.
- **Low-friction lead capture as an alternative to the hard CTA** — e.g., a 60-second quiz/assessment — captures people not ready to book a call (Healthy Parent Club's 60-sec health quiz cited as a smart low-friction mechanism).

### 3B — Hook / headline formulas that convert
[T2 — SeedProd, Unbounce, KlientBoost, Instapage, 2026]
Highest-reliability formulas, all outcome-led:
1. **Outcome + time anchor** — "[Outcome] in [time]." Time anchors make an outcome feel real.
2. **Outcome without the common pain** — "How to [outcome] without [pain]."
3. **Specific outcome + credibility** — outcome + number/proof marker.
Principles: name a specific outcome/audience/problem; avoid generic adjectives; spell out the exact outcome in plain language; specificity (a real number, a real situation) beats vague aspiration.
Note for this brand: the locked hero line **"Stop guessing. Follow a system."** is a *positioning* line, not an outcome line. Best practice → pair it with an outcome-led overline/subhead so the cold visitor gets both the emotional hook *and* the concrete "what changes for me."

### 3C — Minimalist-editorial design as a conversion tool (not just aesthetics)
[T2 — DigitalSilk, Typza, DesignStudio, Inkbot, 2026]
- 2026 minimalism = "intentional storytelling shaped by structure, contrast and flow" — space, typography and hierarchy guide attention deliberately.
- Typography increasingly carries brand identity and emotional tone; refined serif headings signal editorial polish, authority, thought leadership. (Maps to Cormorant Garamond Italic headlines.)
- Whitespace defines structure and hierarchy, helps users process faster, reduces cognitive load → faster conversions.
- "Functional minimalism": every element must serve a measurable purpose; purposeful negative space directs attention.
This is direct external validation of the brand's locked rule **"Structure is the design"** and the **No-Line Rule** — the brand system is not just on-aesthetic, it's on-conversion.

### 3D — PH web & payment behavior
[T2 — FintechNewsPH, Statista, unbox.ph, Antom, 2026]
- PH e-commerce ~₱2.1T in 2026; ~67.2M active shoppers; digital-first.
- **GCash dominant**: ~38% of e-commerce payments / ~89% digital-wallet share. **Maya** second (~16%). Cash-on-delivery falling to ~11%.
- Mobile wallets are the preferred online payment method, especially for Millennials — overtaking cards.
- Implication for the site: mobile-first is non-negotiable; when payment is collected (post-call enrollment), **GCash/Maya must be first-class options** and shown as trust signals. The booking/lead-capture step itself should be ultra-light (wallet trust matters at the payment step, not the booking step).

## Layer 4 — Competitive Intel [COMPLETE]

### 4A — Web structure of strong global coaching sites
[T2 — Hostinger, mypersonaltrainerwebsite, 2026]
- **Ultimate Performance** — hero opens with a bold results claim, fast load, clean structure, zero clutter. Lesson: confidence + speed + no clutter. (Don't copy the specific % claim.)
- **The Body Coach / Joe Wicks** — total coherence across color, tone, imagery, CTA language; the content ecosystem (app, social, programs) funnels back to the site; homepage makes "who it's for" and "what to do next" obvious even at huge scale. Lesson: web ↔ social coherence is a competitive moat — exactly Jap's "content must complement social" goal.
- **Healthy Parent Club** — 60-second quiz as low-friction capture; specific, emotionally credible testimonials. Lesson: offer a soft on-ramp beside the hard CTA.

### 4B — Standard winning homepage skeleton (to compress, not copy)
[T2 — advertaimarketing "7 homepage sections", multiple 2026 sources]
Typical full structure: hero (problem/goal headline) → who I am (photo + 2–3 sentences) → services grid → transformation/testimonial proof → clear offer with enquiry path → friction-free CTA on every page. This is the 7–8 section pattern — the same one the current Restart schema uses. The 4-section page compresses this by *merging* (problem+system into one, proof+offer into one), not by deleting persuasion steps.

### 4C — PH market gap (web specifically)
[T2 — corroborates story-content-dm-strategy Layer 4]
PH fitness web presence skews aesthetics/motivation-led, cluttered, with offers buried and generic copy. The unclaimed web position: **minimalist-editorial, system-led, proof-early, one clear action, mobile-tight.** The brand file already specifies this aesthetic — the competitive insight is that it's also the conversion-optimal choice in this market, so there's no tension between "on-brand" and "high-converting."

## Layer 5 — Hypothesis [COMPLETE]

### Core hypothesis
A tight 4-section homepage will out-convert the 8-section schema for cold social traffic **if** each section carries a full persuasion beat (not a deleted one) and proof appears early. The 4 sections map cleanly onto the proven Curiosity → Empathy → Safety/Desire → Action arc, and each section's headline doubles as a social hook — making the website the anchor the social calendar repurposes from, not a separate asset.

### The 4-Section Homepage Blueprint (working model — to refine with Jap, then route to web-copy.md)

```
SECTION 1 — THE HOOK (Curiosity + early proof)   [persuasion: Curiosity → Safety primer]
- Overline: outcome-led, niche-specific (e.g., "Structured online coaching")
- Hero headline: outcome-led line OR the brand line "Stop guessing. Follow a system."
  → Best practice: brand line as headline + outcome-led subhead beneath it
- Subhead: 25–35 words — names who it's for (people, not "busy adults") + what changes
- Primary CTA: Book a Discovery Call   |   Transitional: DM "RESTART"
- Thin proof strip directly under the fold: 3–4 specific markers (years coaching,
  clients coached, real result) — PROOF EARLY, no invented numbers
- Must be readable + CTA visible without scrolling on mobile

SECTION 2 — PROBLEM → SYSTEM (Empathy → Safety)  [merges schema sections 3+4]
- Empathy first: name the real problem (not effort — structure; random workouts;
  guessing on nutrition; quitting at week 3). No shame, no hype.
- Bridge: "Not a gym. A coaching system."
- The Restart Fitness System: 4 pillars compressed (Training · Nutrition · Habits ·
  Accountability) — 1 line each, text blocks (NO three-icon cliché row)

SECTION 3 — PROOF + OFFER PATH (Safety + Desire)  [merges schema sections 5+7]
- Specific client proof (real name/alias, starting point, change, timeframe — confirmed only)
- The path to start: route to the offer. One primary action — not all 5 tiers stacked.
  Likely: lead with the discovery call as the "we'll find your fit" step, with
  6FIX / 90-Day as the visible on-ramps (pull names/positioning from offer-engine.md)

SECTION 4 — LEAD CAPTURE (Action)                [replaces 8-section "final CTA"]
- Direct, practical headline (not motivational)
- Primary: Book a Discovery Call  |  Transitional: DM "RESTART"
- RECOMMENDED: gate the call behind a 2–4 field qualifier (application-first model)
  rather than a raw calendar — 30–55% vs <5% conversion. Soft fallback = DM "RESTART".
```

### Strategic recommendations (decisions for Jap)
1. **Adopt application-first booking.** Replace "raw Calendly" with a short qualifier (goal, current situation, timeline → then calendar). This is the single highest-leverage finding and it resolves Open Decision #1 (booking method) in a data-backed direction. Low-readiness leads route to DM "RESTART" instead.
2. **Hero = brand line + outcome subhead.** Keep "Stop guessing. Follow a system." as the identity headline; add an outcome-led subhead so cold visitors get the concrete "what changes for me."
3. **Proof early.** Move a thin proof strip to directly under the hero, not just a section near the bottom. (Requires confirmed numbers — see gaps.)
4. **One action per section; two CTAs max on the page** (Book a Discovery Call + DM "RESTART"). Confirmed by both web research and the existing story-content file.
5. **Lean into the brand aesthetic as conversion strategy** — minimalist editorial / "Structure is the design" is validated as conversion-optimal AND unclaimed in PH. No tradeoff to make here.
6. **Build web hooks as social-ready lines** — each section headline should survive as a standalone Threads/IG hook, so the social calendar repurposes the site (Joe Wicks coherence model).

### Content seeds (pre-routed)
- **Web copy (web-copy.md):** 4-section homepage section copy + hooks; then About, Programs path, Results, Book.
- **Social (social-post.md / campaign.md):** each of the 4 section headlines → a feed-post hook; the Problem→System beat ties to the 5 story types already defined in story-content-dm-strategy.
- **Design (design-brief.md / ui-ux-pro-max):** 4-section layout with proof-early hero, mobile-tight fold, whitespace-driven section separation (No-Line Rule).
