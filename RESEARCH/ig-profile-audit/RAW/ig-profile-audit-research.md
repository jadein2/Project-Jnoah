## LAYER 2 — AUDIT FINDINGS

### @restartfitnessph — Critical Issues

**1. Following > Followers (73 followers, 76 following)**
The brand account is following more accounts than follow it. For a cold visitor this is a credibility kill — it reads as an account that has not yet earned an audience. This is often the first number people check when deciding to follow a business account.

**2. Bio is cut off and has no CTA visible**
The visible bio fragment ends at "Training, Nutrition, Habits & ..." — the DM "RESTART" CTA and the core promise are buried in the overflow. Cold visitors on mobile see only 2 lines before "more." The hook and action must live in those first 2 lines.

**3. Link points to Urban Strong, not Restart Fitness PH**
`www.urbanstrongfit.com/online-coaching` sends every click off the Restart Fitness PH brand and onto a different brand. This is a leakage problem — the CTA pipeline breaks at the link click. Should point to restartfitnessph.com (once live) or the Discovery Call booking page directly.

**4. Too many Highlights — wrong labels**
Six highlights is too many for a focused brand. The campaign brief specifies 4 buckets: System · Results · Real Coaching · About Jap. Current highlights have "In-Person" (Urban Strong offer, not Restart PH) and "Program Based" (confusing label). Discovery Calls is correct but should be "Book a Call" or "Start Here."

**5. "Personal coach" in bio is generic**
The brand line is "Stop guessing. Follow a system." None of that is visible. "Personal coach" tells a cold visitor nothing distinctive about what Restart Fitness PH does differently.

**6. 99 posts with 73 followers = content not converting to follows**
High post count, very low follow conversion rate. This signals either: the content isn't being discovered (reach problem), or it's being discovered but not convincing enough to follow (content quality/hook problem), or both. The grid is not doing its job as a conversion tool.

---

### @japhetfelipe — Issues

**1. Link mismatch**
`www.urbanstrongfit.com/online` — same brand confusion. If the personal account is the discovery layer that feeds @restartfitnessph, the link should go to the Restart Fitness PH booking page or at minimum tag the brand account clearly in the CTA.

**2. Highlights are mixed — personal and professional without structure**
"Boggy" (personal — pet/person?) and "CrossFit Things" sit next to "Online Coaching" and "Coaching." No visitor can tell what this account is for from the highlights alone. The personal content is good — it builds personality — but it needs to be framed so the professional purpose is still clear.

**3. Bio underuses CF-L2 and 10+ years**
CF-L2 Trainer and 10+ years of experience are strong credibility signals. They're in the bio but not positioned as the reason to pay attention. "Japhet Felipe | Personal Trainer & Coach | CF-L2 Trainer | 10+ Years Coaching Expertise" is a resume, not a hook.

**4. 187 posts with 318 followers — better but still low**
Better ratio than the brand account, but 187 posts with 318 followers means the account's discovery mechanism is not working well either. Content is being posted but reach is limited.

**5. Cross-promotion is directional but unclear**
The bio mentions @restartfitnessph but it's buried after the credential list. The average cold visitor won't make the connection clearly enough to follow both accounts.

## LAYER 3 — PLATFORM INTELLIGENCE (2026)

### Grid Format Change (Critical)
Instagram changed profile grid thumbnails from square (1:1) to vertical (3:4) in early 2025. This means all grid planning must account for vertical thumbnails. The safe zone for text and faces is the center of a 3:4 frame.

**Recommended post format:** 4:5 (1080 × 1350px). This maximizes both the in-feed display and the grid thumbnail with no cropping issues.

### Top-3 Tile Impact
- A visitor forms a first impression in ~0.2 seconds
- The top 3 tiles (first row) is the first thing seen on a profile visit
- Most follow/no-follow decisions happen before scrolling
- These 3 tiles must collectively answer: Who is this for? What do they get? Why follow?

### Format Performance (2026 data)
| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Carousel | 10% / 1.92% avg | Highest engagement. First slide = 80% of the weight. Hook must earn the swipe. |
| Single Image | 7% | Visual anchor, fast scroll stop |
| Reel | 6% | Discovery engine — reach cold audiences |

Carousel + comment-to-DM automation converts at 12–18% vs. bio link at 2–3%.

### What the Top 3 Tiles Should Do (Coach/Fitness Account)
- **Tile 1 (left):** Reach and hook — Reel or a carousel with a strong first-slide hook. Cold audience entry point. Must work with sound off and in a 3:4 thumbnail.
- **Tile 2 (center):** System or proof — shows what you actually do/deliver. Carousel or single image.
- **Tile 3 (right):** Human/connection — Coach Jap the person, a real moment, a direct-to-camera caption. Reduces "is this a real person?" friction.

Planning rule: design all 3 as a visual unit first (same background tone, same typography treatment) before producing them individually.

## LAYER 4 — PERSONAL vs. BRAND ACCOUNT ARCHITECTURE

The correct structure for a two-account setup (founder + brand):

```
@japhetfelipe (Personal)
  → Purpose: Trust, personality, credibility, backstory
  → Tone: Rawer, more conversational, training moments, coaching philosophy
  → CTA: "I coach through @restartfitnessph — DM 'RESTART' or visit the link"
  → Grid feel: Real, editorial, slightly less produced
  → Link: Discovery Call booking page (same as brand account)

@restartfitnessph (Brand)
  → Purpose: System, proof, conversion
  → Tone: Polished, direct, brand-consistent
  → CTA: DM "RESTART"
  → Grid feel: Consistent brand palette, designed tiles
  → Link: restartfitnessph.com (or booking page until site is live)
```

The personal account builds the person. The brand account sells the system. Neither should try to do both fully. Cold visitors who find @japhetfelipe first should be able to understand within 3 seconds: "This guy is a serious coach — follow @restartfitnessph for the program."

## LAYER 5 — TOP-3 TILE RECOMMENDATIONS

### @restartfitnessph — First Row Design Direction

**Option A: Problem → System → Proof**
| Position | Format | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left (Tile 1) | Reel or Carousel | "Most people don't need more motivation. They need a plan that works." Hook-first, teaches one thing, ends with DM "RESTART" | Cold reach, discovery |
| Center (Tile 2) | Carousel (4–6 slides) | "The Restart Fitness System — 4 pillars, one clear plan" | System explanation, saves |
| Right (Tile 3) | Single image | Client win or coaching moment — real, specific, brief | Social proof, human element |

**Option B: Authority → Problem → CTA**
| Position | Format | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left (Tile 1) | Single image | Bold statement card: "Stop guessing. Follow a system." — charcoal background, gold accent, Cormorant headline | Visual brand anchor |
| Center (Tile 2) | Carousel | "4 reasons people stop training after 2 weeks — and what fixes each one" | Educational, saveable |
| Right (Tile 3) | Reel | Coach Jap direct-to-camera — 20–30 seconds, one idea, one CTA | Human, reach |

**Recommended: Option A.** Reel on left = discovery. Carousel in center = depth. Proof on right = trust. This sequence mirrors the C/C/V framework: Value → Value → Conversion.

### @japhetfelipe — First Row Design Direction

| Position | Format | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left (Tile 1) | Single image or Reel | Coach Jap origin story or training session — candid, real | Who is this person? |
| Center (Tile 2) | Single image | Coaching philosophy statement — one clear belief about fitness/coaching | What does he stand for? |
| Right (Tile 3) | Carousel or Single | Tag to @restartfitnessph — "I coach here" moment, or client win with brand mention | Where to go next |

### Visual Design Rules for Top-3 Tiles (Both Accounts)

**@restartfitnessph grid:**
- Background: Charcoal (#1A1A18) or off-white (#F5F5F0) — alternate by row for rhythm
- Headline font: Cormorant Garamond Italic (large, editorial)
- Body/caption font: DM Sans
- Accent: Burnt orange (#E06D22) for CTA elements, gold (#C9A84C) for decorative lines
- No gradients, no drop shadows, no stock photo backgrounds
- Format: 1080 × 1350px (4:5), designed to 3:4 grid thumbnail

**@japhetfelipe grid:**
- Same font DNA (Cormorant + DM Sans) but applied looser — less structured
- Real photos dominant, text overlays minimal
- Color treatment matches @restartfitnessph when posting brand-linked content
- Personal moments (training, candid) in natural color — no forced brand palette
