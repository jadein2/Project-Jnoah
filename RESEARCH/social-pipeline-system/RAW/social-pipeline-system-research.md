## Layer 2 — Hard Evidence [COMPLETE]

### 2A — Pipeline conversion benchmarks

**DM inquiry rate**
[T2 — industry benchmark, multiple coaching platform sources]
A functional content pipeline should generate >3% DM inquiry rate from profile visitors. Of those DMs, >15% should book a consultation. Coaches below these thresholds have a content-to-DM gap (content isn't creating enough qualified intent) or a DM-to-call gap (conversations aren't converting).

**Discovery call conversion rate**
[T2 — coaching industry benchmarks, Paperbell, Fitbudd, multiple sources]
Average discovery call close rate across coaching categories: 10–30%. Structured discovery call scripts and pre-call prep sequences lift close rate by approximately 35%. Unstructured calls (no agenda, no qualification pre-call) are at the bottom of that range. Structured calls with a pre-call form are at the top.

**Lead nurture timing — how long it actually takes**
[T2 — B2C coaching funnel data, multiple sources, 2025]
On average, 8 touches are required before a lead books a call. 50% of leads who DM are qualified but not ready to buy at the moment of contact. Nurtured leads are 47% more likely to purchase than non-nurtured leads. Optimal welcome sequence: 3–7 messages over 7–14 days. Implication: most coaches stop after 1–2 follow-ups and leave half their revenue on the table.

**Structured onboarding → retention**
[T2 — Trainerize blog, TrueCoach, 2025]
Structured onboarding programs (defined first-week experience, clear expectations set) produce 28% higher retention rates and 35% higher client satisfaction scores compared to unstructured "just start the program" onboarding. Most client drop-off in online coaching occurs in the first 60 days — the exact window where the onboarding system either holds or loses them.

**Pre-call no-show reduction**
[T2 — booking platform data, Calendly, Acuity, coaching operations sources]
Pre-call confirmation sequences (email confirmation + prep question + 24h reminder) reduce no-shows by 30–50%. Automated reminder systems alone cut no-shows by approximately half. Practical rule: do not book discovery calls more than 2 weeks in advance — leads go cold faster than the calendar moves.

**Lost lead re-engagement timing**
[T2 — coaching sales data, multiple sources]
Most coaching sales happen between the 5th and 12th contact. Standard re-engagement protocol: touch at Day 7, Day 14, Day 30. The framing at each touchpoint is "not no, not right now" — the lead wasn't ready, not uninterested. Major re-approach (new angle, new offer framing) should wait 4–6 weeks from last contact.

**Content-to-DM pipeline structure**
[T2 — social media coaching guides, Fitbudd Academy, 2025–2026]
Platform role by funnel stage:
- Reels/short video → top-of-funnel (awareness, reach, searchability)
- Carousels/educational posts → middle (authority, consideration)
- Stories → connection layer (retention of warm audience, soft accountability)
- DM CTA → bottom (conversion trigger)
DM response time matters: coaches who respond within 24 hours maintain conversion; leads who don't get a response within 24 hours are significantly more likely to move on.

**DM qualification — first message rules**
[T2 — sales training, coaching DM guides, 2025]
Effective first DM qualification: maximum 2 questions. No pitch in first message. Frame the discovery call as a "roadmap session" or "fit call" — not as a sales call. Lead qualification should surface goal, timeline, and prior attempts before any offer is mentioned.

## Layer 3 — Social and Trend Patterns [COMPLETE]

### 3A — DM automation tools (what's actually being used)

**ManyChat — dominant tool for coaches selling through calls**
[T2 — ManyChat documentation, Intellicoach analysis, 2025–2026]
ManyChat is the most widely used DM automation tool for online fitness coaches. Key capabilities: Instagram keyword triggers (comment or DM a word → automated response fires), multi-step DM sequences, qualifier branching. Cost: $14–29/month. Coaches report response time drops to near-instant (99% faster than manual). Engagement lift: up to 40%. Documented outcome from coaching case studies: coaches scaling from 40 to 120+ monthly booked calls after implementing keyword triggers. Trigger word "RESTART" is exactly the format ManyChat is built for — one word, DM trigger, opens a sequence.

**Tool landscape for coaches at Restart PH's scale**
[T2 — Intellicoach, Inrō, CreatorFlow, MaaDiy, 2025–2026]
Other tools visible in the competitive landscape:
- Inrō — tags, qualifies, follows up with engaged followers; slightly more advanced segmentation
- CreatorFlow — free plan (500 DMs/month), paid from $15/month; simpler setup
- MaaDiy — comment-to-DM focus, turns posts into lead magnets
- Intellicoach — built specifically for fitness coaches; claims 35–45% conversion from DM to booked call
ManyChat remains the most commonly cited by coaches at the 100–500 follower conversion range.

**Fragmented tool stacks — where most coaches are operating**
[T2 — GoHighLevel analysis, Paperbell, coaching operations sources, 2025–2026]
Most coaches at sub-$20K/month revenue are running disconnected stacks: Calendly or Acuity for booking ($10–25/month) + Mailchimp or ConvertKit for email ($30–100/month) + Typeform or Google Forms for intake + spreadsheets for tracking. Result: leads fall through the cracks between tools. There is no single view of where every lead sits in the pipeline. This is the most common structural weakness visible across coaching businesses at this stage.

**Algorithm patterns — what's being rewarded in 2025–2026**
[T2 — Instagram/TikTok algorithm analysis, Fitbudd, 2026]
Instagram algorithm rewards saves, shares, and DMs — not just likes. Content that generates DMs gets pushed to more accounts. Reels remain top-of-funnel. Instagram Profile Shop tab launched July 2025 — allows direct purchase from profile without leaving the app (relevant for 6Fix direct enrollment). 74% of Gen Z now use TikTok as a search engine — important if the pipeline expands to TikTok later.

### 3B — Discovery call and booking patterns

**Pre-call sequence structure**
[T2 — coaching operations guides, Heyflow, 2025–2026]
High-converting discovery call sequences:
1. Booking confirmation (immediate) → link + what to expect
2. Pre-call prep form (1–3 questions about goals and biggest challenge) → sent at booking
3. Reminder (24h before) → brief, no pressure, includes link
4. Reminder (1h before, optional) → increases show rate further
The prep form serves dual purpose: reduces no-shows (invested lead is more likely to show) and pre-qualifies the lead so the call can skip the basic qualification phase.

**Rolling cohort model for 6Fix**
[T2 — Trainerize business models blog, 2026]
Programs with a specific promised outcome running on defined cohort starts generate more predictable monthly revenue than open-enrollment models. Implications for 6Fix: a defined start date and cohort framing ("the next group starts [date]") creates urgency without false scarcity. Also enables a pipeline report: how many leads are currently in pre-enrollment vs. in-program vs. post-program.

### 3C — Onboarding and retention patterns

**First 60 days are the entire retention battle**
[T2 — Trainerize blog, TrueCoach, Australian Institute of Fitness, 2025–2026]
Industry-wide: 60% of online fitness clients disengage within 90 days. The vast majority of that drop-off happens in the first 60 days. A 6-week program (42 days) runs entirely within this window. Structured onboarding that sets clear expectations for Week 1 prevents the ambiguity that causes early exits.

**Connection layer must run parallel to program delivery**
[T2 — Trainerize survey, 2025]
57% of clients cite social interaction and connection as a primary reason they stay — not results alone. Trainerize delivers the program. The social layer (Stories, polls, coach visibility) delivers the connection. Both must run simultaneously for full retention effect.

## Layer 4 — Competitive Intel [COMPLETE]

### 4A — What top coaches are doing visibly

**ManyChat keyword triggers are now table stakes**
[T2 — Intellicoach competitive analysis, Fitbudd, Inrō, 2025–2026]
The most visible pipeline feature across competitive online fitness coaches is the keyword DM trigger. Trigger words observed: TRANSFORM, MEALPLAN, CHALLENGE, RESET, START, FREE. The pattern is standardized: post or Reel CTA says "DM me [WORD]" → auto-response fires immediately → sequence opens with 1–2 qualification questions → coach picks up from there or routes to booking. Coaches without this are visibly slower to respond and lose leads to coaches who respond in seconds.

**Qualification script — observed across multiple coaches**
[T2 — observed across coaching Instagram accounts and sales training content, 2025]
Standard qualification sequence visible in competitor pipelines:
1. "What's your main goal right now — [weight loss / build muscle / general fitness]?"
2. "What's the biggest thing that's gotten in the way before?"
3. "Can I share a quick overview of how I work with people? If there's a fit, I'd love to get you on a 20-min roadmap call."
No pricing mentioned until after the call. Discovery call is always framed as a diagnostic, not a sales meeting.

**Where coaches are visibly losing leads**
[T2 — intellicoach.ai, andrewwallis.com, exercise.com, 2025]
Observed failure patterns across coaching pipelines:
- Slow DM response (manual reply during business hours only) → leads go to faster-responding competitors
- No re-engagement after 1 touchpoint → 50% of addressable revenue left uncaptured
- Booking pages with friction (long forms, multiple steps, no confirmation sequence) → elevated no-shows
- Non-converting websites (no clear CTA, testimonials buried, program details vague) → site visits don't convert
- No pipeline visibility → coaches don't know which leads are warm without manually scrolling DMs
- Content that generates curiosity but not qualified intent → DM volume without booking volume

**All-in-one stack vs. pieced-together tools**
[T2 — GoHighLevel, Paperbell, Exercise.com, 2025–2026]
Coaches at $10K–$30K/month are moving toward all-in-one platforms (GoHighLevel at $97/month covers CRM, booking, automation, email, pipeline view). Coaches below that threshold are still running fragmented stacks. The competitive gap: an all-in-one stack makes it near-impossible for a lead to fall through — every action triggers a follow-up. Restart PH's current stack (Trainerize + Wix + manual DMs) has gaps between all three tools where leads can disappear.

## Layer 5 — Hypothesis [COMPLETE]

### Restart PH-specific pipeline architecture

Based on all five layers, here is the pipeline as it should run for Restart PH — stage by stage, with automation vs. personal notes at each step.

```
STAGE 1 — AWARENESS (Content Engine)
→ Reels: problem-based, outcome-led, searchable (top-of-funnel reach)
→ Stories: 3–5 daily, coach presence, poll accountability (warm audience retention)
→ Carousels: educational authority content (middle-of-funnel consideration)
→ All CTAs route to DM "RESTART"

STAGE 2 — FIRST CONTACT (ManyChat — Automate)
→ "RESTART" keyword triggers instant DM sequence
→ Message 1: acknowledge + 1 qualification question (goal)
→ Message 2 (if reply received): 1 follow-up question (biggest obstacle)
→ Message 3: offer roadmap call — "I'd love to get you on a 20-min call to map this out. Here's my booking link: [Wix Bookings link]"
→ Jap picks up conversation manually if lead engages beyond the sequence

STAGE 3 — WARM HOLD (Semi-automated, 7–14 days)
→ Leads who don't book: Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14 follow-up via DM
→ Framing: check-in, not pitch ("Hey — just checking back in, any questions about how this works?")
→ After Day 14 with no response: move to lost lead re-engagement protocol (Day 30 touchpoint)

STAGE 4 — BOOKING (Wix — Automate)
→ Lead clicks Wix Bookings link → selects Discovery Call slot
→ Immediate confirmation email fires (Wix automation)
→ Pre-call prep form: 2 questions (primary goal, biggest obstacle) — reduces no-shows + pre-qualifies
→ 24h reminder email fires automatically
→ 1h reminder (optional, set up if possible in Wix)
→ Rule: don't accept bookings more than 2 weeks out

STAGE 5 — DISCOVERY CALL (Personal — Jap)
→ discovery-call.md engine handles call structure
→ Pre-call form data reviewed before call starts
→ Route to: 6Fix (₱4,500) → Restart-P (₱2,500–3,500/mo) → Restart-90 (₱18,000)
→ Close on call or within 24h follow-up
→ Target: 25–35% close rate (currently unknown — [GAP: baseline needed])

STAGE 6 — ENROLLMENT + ONBOARDING (Wix + Trainerize — Automate first week)
→ Payment via Wix Bookings or direct transfer
→ Wix: onboarding confirmation email fires immediately
→ Trainerize: program assigned, habits configured, auto-messages activated
→ Auto-message cadence (6Fix): Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 14 / Day 21 / Day 35 / Day 42
→ [GAP: confirm whether this cadence is currently configured in Trainerize]
→ Structured first week: clear what to do on Day 1 (reduce early exit ambiguity)

STAGE 7 — ACTIVE PROGRAM (Trainerize + Social — Mixed)
→ Trainerize: workout delivery, habit tracking, weekly check-in form
→ Auto-messages at scheduled intervals (automated)
→ Weekly video feedback from Jap (personal — non-negotiable)
→ Social: daily Stories presence, poll accountability, progress celebrations with permission (personal)
→ check-in.md engine: intervention protocol if client misses check-in in weeks 2–4

STAGE 8 — CLOSE + UPGRADE (Personal — Jap)
→ Week 6: review message or call
→ sales-engine.md: upgrade DM flows for 6Fix → Restart-P or → Restart-90
→ offer-engine.md: upgrade pricing and path logic
→ Post-program: follow up at Day 7 after close ("How's week 7 going?")

STAGE 9 — LOST LEAD RE-ENGAGEMENT (ManyChat + Manual — Automate where possible)
→ Did not book after DM: Day 30 re-engagement ("We're starting a new 6Fix group [date] — want to jump in?")
→ Did not close after call: Day 7 follow-up ("Did you get a chance to think about it?") → Day 14 → Day 30
→ Closed program, did not upgrade: Day 14 after close ("How's week 7 treating you?")
→ Most sales happen between 5th–12th contact — treat lost leads as warm, not cold
```
