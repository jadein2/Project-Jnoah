## Layer 3 — Social and Trend Patterns [COMPLETE]

### 3A — Trainerize platform capabilities (what it actually does for a 6-week delivery)

**Master Habit Programs**
[T2 — Trainerize documentation, 2025]
Trainerize supports Master Habit Programs that can be built once and assigned to multiple clients simultaneously. Duration is configurable — 4, 6, or 12 weeks. Coach can monitor habit completion across all clients via a dashboard and identify who needs intervention without manual check-ins. This directly supports a scalable 6Fix delivery where Jap runs multiple clients at the same time without individual manual tracking.

**Auto-Messages (Program-Scheduled)**
[T2 — Trainerize Help Center documentation]
Auto-messages can be embedded into a Master Program and scheduled by day number (e.g., Day 1 welcome, Day 7 first check-in prompt, Day 21 halfway message, Day 35 final week). They deliver straight to the client's app at the scheduled time. Repeat options available. This means a 6Fix program can have a full 42-day message cadence built in — no manual sending required once the program is set up. Currently unknown whether this is being used in the existing 6Fix program. [GAP — confirm whether auto-messages are configured in current 6Fix Trainerize setup]

**Hybrid delivery model — dominant pattern**
[T2 — Trainerize internal data, 2025]
70%+ of Trainerize coaches now run hybrid businesses (in-person + online + async). This is the direction the platform is optimized for. Confirms 6Fix as program-only (async online) is a supported and growing model — not an edge case.

**AI-assisted operations**
[T2 — Trainerize blog, 2026]
56% of coaches already use AI to support programming, nutrition, messaging, and operations. Platform is investing in this direction (2026 roadmap). Directional signal only at this stage — watch for Trainerize AI features that could auto-flag clients who miss workouts.

**Client stay for connection, not just fitness**
[T2 — Trainerize internal survey data, 2025]
57% of clients cite social interaction and connection as a primary reason they remain in a fitness community — not results alone. This is the single most important pattern for 6Fix: the program's fitness delivery may be solid, but if the connection layer is thin, clients disengage before week 6.

**60% quit in 90 days**
[T2 — Trainerize blog, 2026]
Industry pattern: 60% of online fitness clients disengage within 90 days. A 6-week (42-day) program falls entirely within this highest-risk window. Weeks 2–5 are the dropout danger zone. The structure of 6Fix (6 weeks, weekly check-ins) covers the accountability layer — but without an active connection layer running in parallel, drop-off risk stays high.

**Community and accountability — key levers**
[T2 — TrueCoach, Everfit, Trainerize blog — multiple sources, 2025–2026]
Consistent across multiple coaching platforms: clients who feel connected to others (peers or coach) are more consistent, more motivated, and far less likely to quit. Pattern appears across 3+ independent sources → approaching T1 weight for content and system decisions.
- Regular check-ins (weekly minimum) → confirmed
- Community peer accountability → boosts completion
- Progress visibility (photos, metrics, personal bests) → reinforces momentum
- Recognition (celebrating wins publicly) → drives retention and referrals

### 3B — Social media role within an active program cycle (not just marketing)

**Stories are the daily coaching presence tool**
[T2 — Fitbudd Academy, 2025 / Trainerize social media guide, 2026]
Optimal posting during an active coaching cycle: 3–5 daily Stories, 4–5 feed posts per week, 3–4 Reels per week. Stories function as lo-fi, casual, coach-voice daily touchpoints — not polished content. During a 6Fix cycle, stories serve as the coach showing up every day even when there's no 1:1 interaction. This is the connection layer that Trainerize alone does not provide.

**30% of content should be community-building during active programs**
[T2 — 2025 Fitness Content Effectiveness Report, cited in Trainerize blog]
The effective content mix for coaches with active programs: ~30% community-building content (Q&As, polls, challenges, interactive discussions). This is not marketing content — it is retention content aimed at current clients who follow the coach on social. Single source — use as directional, not rule.

**Poll-based accountability via Stories**
[T3 — coaching industry observation, multiple Instagram accounts]
Coaches running active cohorts use IG story polls as soft public accountability: "Did you train today? Yes / Rest day." Current clients see these, answer them, and feel seen. Creates a lightweight community layer that supplements the 1:1 Trainerize check-in. Observed pattern across multiple coaches — not formally researched.

**Progress celebration as retention and acquisition**
[T2 — TrueCoach, Australian Institute of Fitness, multiple coaching blogs — 2025]
Sharing client progress (with permission) on social creates two effects simultaneously: retention (current clients feel recognized) and acquisition (cold audience sees proof). Pattern appears across multiple independent sources. Within a 6Fix cycle, a week 3 or week 5 progress post for an active client serves both purposes.

**Facebook for community, Instagram for acquisition and authority**
[T2 — Trainerize social media guide, GymMaster, 2025–2026]
Platform role split that appears consistently: Instagram for authority building, reach, and cold lead conversion; Facebook for community, warm audience, and group-based accountability. If 6Fix clients are in a private Facebook Group, that functions as a community layer. [GAP — confirm whether a 6Fix Facebook Group exists or is planned]

**Interactive programs with clear outcomes generate predictable revenue**
[T2 — Trainerize blog on fitness business models, 2026]
Programs with a specific promised outcome ("Build your first pull-up in 21 days" / by extension "Build your first layer of structure in 6 weeks") run on rolling cohorts — new group starts on a defined schedule, generates predictable monthly revenue. Confirms that 6Fix as a rolling-cohort model is viable and aligns with what's working in the market right now.

### 3C — Wix role in the coaching system

**Wix Bookings — native, no third-party required**
[T2 — Wix documentation, 2025]
Wix has native booking built in: clients can book, pay, and manage appointments directly on the site. Syncs with Google, Outlook, and Apple Calendar. Accepts one-time payments and recurring payments. For 6Fix, this means a dedicated enrollment page with a "Enroll in 6Fix" button that collects payment and books the onboarding call without requiring a separate tool. **Wix as trust hub before enrollment**

For coaching brands, the website functions as a trust-building layer between first social contact and enrollment. Blog content (SEO), coach bio (authority), and social proof (testimonials) all operate here. The client journey: social post → DM → site visit → enroll or book call. Wix does this well with existing built-in tools.

[T2 — Wix documentation, 2025]
Wix allows automated email communication triggered by client actions (booking confirmation, reminder, post-session follow-up). For 6Fix: automated onboarding email on enrollment, week 3 check-in reminder email, week 6 "what's next" upgrade prompt. ### 3D — Full client journey pattern (observed across successful online coaches)

Based on researched patterns, the highest-performing online coaching delivery systems follow this structure:

```
AWARENESS
Social content (feed posts + Reels) → profile visit → bio link → site

INTEREST
Wix site → coach bio + program page + testimonials → clear CTA

DECISION
Book Discovery Call (Wix Bookings) → 20-30 min call → 6Fix enrollment → payment via Wix

ONBOARDING
Trainerize: client added, program assigned, habits configured, auto-messages set
Wix: automated enrollment confirmation email
Social: DM welcome or story shoutout (with permission)

DELIVERY (Weeks 1–6)
Trainerize: workout delivery, habit tracking, weekly check-in form
Auto-messages: Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 14 / Day 21 / Day 35 / Day 42
Coach: weekly video feedback (per 6Fix promise), in-app messaging
Social: daily Stories presence, poll-based accountability, progress celebrations

CLOSE
Week 6 review call or message
Assessment: what worked, what didn't, what's next
Upgrade path: → Restart-P (most common) or → Restart-90 (transformation goal)
Trainerize: new program assigned or subscription started

POST-PROGRAM
Social: follow-up content for upgrade decision
Wix: upgrade landing page or direct Restart-P enrollment
```

[T3 — synthesized from multiple coaching patterns — directional framework, not a single sourced model]
