# Anti-AI Output Rules

Global filter. Applies to every piece of client-facing copy and every design output. No exceptions.

Load this whenever producing copy, captions, blogs, DMs, web text, slide content, ad copy, email subject lines — anything Jap might publish.

---

## Banned words and phrases

**Hard ban — never use:**

*Generic AI tells:*
- tailored
- elevate / elevated / elevating
- unlock / unleash
- embark
- leverage (as a verb)
- delve / delving
- robust
- vibrant
- tapestry
- intricate
- bespoke
- curated
- seamlessly / seamless
- empower / empowering
- transformative
- holistic (unless quoting source)
- synergy
- game-changer
- in today's fast-paced world
- in today's digital age
- it's important to note that
- whether you're a [X] or a [Y]
- imagine this
- the secret to
- this one thing changed everything
- take your [X] to the next level

*Fitness-industry hype (banned across both brands):*
- grind
- destroy
- punish
- no excuses
- just do it
- beast mode
- kill the workout
- shred fast
- fat-burning secrets
- detox
- summer body
- transform your life (as a phrase)

*Restart Fitness PH brand-specific (banned for this brand):*
- so you stop restarting and start progressing
- the last first step
- The Restart Method (use "The Restart Fitness System")
- busy adults / busy people (as headline or audience label)
- restart as a verb in prose

**Use with extreme caution:**
- journey (only when actually appropriate to a long arc)
- transform / transformation (overused in fitness — say "change" or describe the specific result)
- powerful (almost always lazy — replace with the specific thing)
- amazing / incredible / fantastic (replace with what makes it amazing)
- truly (delete it — 95% of the time it adds nothing)
- always / never (avoid fake certainty in coaching claims)
- guaranteed (avoid in coaching outcome claims)

## Sentence patterns to avoid

**The "not just X, but Y" construction.**
- ❌ "Not just a workout, but a lifestyle."
- ✅ "Coaching, programming, accountability — all in one system."

**The triplet for triplet's sake.**
- ❌ "Stronger. Faster. Healthier."
- ✅ Pick one. Make it specific. "Squat 1.5x your bodyweight in 12 weeks."

**Throat-clearing openers.**
- ❌ "In this post, we'll explore..."
- ❌ "Let's talk about..."
- ❌ "Here's the thing —"
- ✅ Open with the point or a concrete image.

**The vague benefit.**
- ❌ "Achieve your goals."
- ✅ "Get your first ring muscle-up by November."

**The hedge.**
- ❌ "This might help you potentially see some results."
- ✅ "Two months in, you'll squat heavier or this didn't work."

**The closing motivational.**
- ❌ "You've got this!" / "Let's go!" / "The journey starts now."
- ✅ End on the CTA or a concrete next step. Or just stop.

## Structural tells of AI copy

Avoid these layouts:

- Three-icon feature row with one-word labels ("Train · Eat · Recover")
- The headline → subhead → three columns → CTA template
- Every paragraph starting with a participial phrase ("Building strength...", "Understanding nutrition...")
- Bullet lists where every bullet is the same length
- Quotation marks around random words for "emphasis"
- Sentences that lead with "Whether you're..."
- The em-dash sandwich on every sentence (use em-dashes when they earn their place, not as filler punctuation)

## What good copy reads like (in Jap's voice)

- Direct. Names the outcome. No throat-clearing.
- Reads like a coach talking to a smart adult, not a brand talking to a customer.
- Specific numbers, specific timeframes, specific movements.
- Calm authority. Confidence without hype.
- One idea per sentence. Multiple short sentences beat one stuffed sentence.
- Uses contractions ("you're," "don't," "it's") — never "you are" in copy.

**Reference cadence:**
> Crash diets work for four weeks. Then your metabolism adapts and the weight comes back. The Restart Method works the other way around — slow first, durable after. Eight weeks in, you're still losing. Twelve weeks in, you're still losing. That's the difference between a diet and a system.

## The pre-publish check

Before any copy ships, scan for:

1. Any banned word above? → kill it
2. Any sentence that could be deleted and not lose meaning? → delete it
3. Any vague verb (achieve, optimize, maximize)? → replace with the specific action
4. Any sentence longer than 25 words? → break it or shorten it
5. Does the first sentence make someone want to read the second? → if not, rewrite
6. Could a competitor's coach put their logo on this and it would still work? → it's too generic, rewrite for Jap's voice

## Anti-AI Writing Protocol — 10 behavioral rules

These go beyond banned words. They govern how copy is constructed. Apply to every client-facing output.

**Rule 1: Start from a real human context.**
Before writing, identify at least one of: real client situation · coach observation · common question · objection heard in real life · mistake people make · personal belief from the coach · pattern seen in training or check-ins. If none exists, ask for one. Do not invent a fabricated scenario and present it as real.

**Rule 2: Avoid generic AI openers.**
Never open with: "In today's fast-paced world" · "Unlock your potential" · "Transform your life" · "Take your fitness journey to the next level" · "Game-changer" · "Elevate your fitness" · "Imagine this" · "The secret to" · "This one thing changed everything."

**Rule 3: Use coach-led language.**
Write like a coach explaining something clearly. Use: "Here is what usually happens." · "This is where people get stuck." · "Start here." · "Do this first." · "The problem is not always effort." · "The better approach is simple."

**Rule 4: Keep imperfection in the voice.**
Human writing has rhythm. Use short lines, occasional direct statements, simple examples, grounded observations, natural transitions. Do not make every paragraph the same length.

**Rule 5: One idea only.**
Every post must have one audience, one problem, one belief shift, one practice, one CTA. AI stacks too many lessons. Do not.

**Rule 6: Add specificity.**
Generic: "Be consistent with your habits." Better: "Pick your first meal before the workday starts."
Generic: "Training helps you feel better." Better: "Three planned sessions remove the daily debate of whether you should train."

**Rule 7: Do not over-polish.**
Avoid making every output sound like a motivational poster. Strong content should feel like a coach talking, a useful reminder, a clear correction, a practical next step.

**Rule 8: Avoid fake certainty.**
Do not overpromise. Avoid: guaranteed transformation · perfect results · always · never · proven to work for everyone. Use: can help · often · most people · a better place to start · a more useful approach.

**Rule 9: Keep CTA natural.**
Bad: "DM RESTART now and change your life forever." Better: "DM 'RESTART' if you want help finding your starting point."

**Rule 10: Final human check.**
Before finalizing, ask: Would a real coach say this? Is there a real observation behind it? Is it too polished? Is it too vague? Does it sound like a person or a template? If it sounds like AI, rewrite with more specificity and less polish.
