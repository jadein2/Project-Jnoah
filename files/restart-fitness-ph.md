## Brand essence

**Brand name:** Restart Fitness PH
**Founder / head coach:** Japhet "Coach Jap" Felipe
**Brand role:** Structured coaching for people who want lasting change, not quick fixes.
**Core line:** Stop guessing. Follow a system.
**Brand essence:** Health is non-negotiable. Fitness protects quality of life. Real progress needs structure.

**Brand philosophy:** Most people don't fail because they lack effort. They fail because they lack structure, standards, feedback, and accountability. Restart Fitness PH exists to provide that system.

## Positioning

**Primary positioning statement:**
Restart Fitness PH is a coaching system for people who want real progress through structure, practical guidance, and accountability.

**Short line:**
Training, nutrition, habits, accountability — coached through a system.

**Business core (use as message anchors):**
- We do not sell access. We sell coaching.
- We do not sell workouts. We sell a system that works when motivation does not.
- Not a gym. A coaching system.
- No hype. No shortcuts. Just a system that gets results.
- The goal is not just results. The goal is results you can keep.

**What the brand is not:**
- A gym-access-only brand
- A random workout page
- A hype-driven fitness brand
- A quick-fix challenge business
- A motivation page
- A brand that leads with weight loss as the main message

## Audience

**Primary age range:** 28–45
**Secondary range:** 35–60 (for health and lifestyle messaging)
**Public label:** **people** — never "busy adults" or "busy people"

**Common profiles:** working professionals, parents, beginners returning to fitness, people who tried programs and stopped, people who need accountability, introverts who prefer guided coaching over crowded group classes, online clients who want a coach-led process.

**Core problems they have:** no clear plan, random workouts, inconsistent nutrition, low accountability, online-fitness confusion, program-hopping after 2–4 weeks, short patience with results, losing weight then regaining it.

**Desired outcomes:** get stronger, feel healthier, improve energy, improve confidence, build consistency, understand what to do, maintain results long-term, lose body fat when relevant to the campaign.

**Emotional drivers:** guided, safe, clear, capable, supported, in control, less confused.

## Coaching philosophy

Health is non-negotiable.

Fitness protects health, improves quality of life, and helps people build better habits.

Training and nutrition are tools. The real transformation comes from structure, consistency, accountability, and better decisions repeated over time.

**Standards (in order):**
- Clarity before intensity
- Fundamentals before complexity
- Structure before motivation
- Accountability before guesswork
- Progress over perfection
- Long-term ownership over short-term dependency

## Language rules

### Always use
people · The Restart Fitness System · The Restart System · structure · system · coaching · accountability · consistency · clarity · fundamentals · practical nutrition · measurable progress · results that stick · real-life fitness · guidance · long-term · standards · habits · coached · progress · sustainable · direction · review · adjust · health · strength · process

### Banned for this brand (in addition to universal anti-AI rules)

- extreme transformation promises, generic fitness-influencer language
- summer body · detox · fat-burning secrets · shred fast · beast mode · kill the workout

## Visual identity

**Design style:** Minimalist editorial.
**Visual feel:** Premium · structured · clean · high contrast · serious but approachable · professional sports editorial · calm authority · fitness-oriented without looking loud.

**Main design rule:** Structure is the design.

### The No-Line Rule

Use spacing, typography scale, contrast, and layout to separate sections — not divider lines, borders, boxes, or visual clutter.

A small 2px orange accent line is allowed only as a controlled brand accent. Never as a decorative divider or section separator.

### Avoid
Clutter · decorative graphics · unnecessary icons · loud effects · neon colors · cheap gym graphics · excessive stickers · overdesigned Canva effects · heavy divider lines · overcrowded layouts · too many fonts · too many colors · **gradients**.

### Color rules (strict)
- Orange — only where action or emphasis is required
- Gold — only for proof, results, authority, or premium signals
- **Never use orange and gold together on the same element**
- Never use pure white `#FFFFFF` (unless a platform technically requires it)
- Never use pure black `#000000`
- Never use orange as a large background
- Never use more than two dominant colors in one visual
- **No gradients ever. Flat color only.**

| Orange on charcoal | Hero sections, social posts, CTA emphasis |
| Gold on charcoal | Results, proof, authority callouts |

## Typography

### Locked font stack

| Role | Font | Style / Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Display / Headlines | **Cormorant Garamond** | **Italic, Light 300** |
| Body / UI / Labels | **DM Sans** | 300, 400, 500 |

### Rules
- Cormorant Garamond Italic is the identity font. Use for headlines, campaign words, pull quotes, section titles.
- **Do not use upright Cormorant for Restart Fitness PH headlines.**
- Do not use DM Sans for major display headlines.
- DM Sans 300 → body paragraphs. DM Sans 400 → general text. DM Sans 500 → buttons, UI labels, overlines, tags, navigation.
- No more than two font weights in a single layout.
- Generous, editorial line height.

### Retired fonts (do not use for Restart Fitness PH)
Playfair Display · Montserrat · Bodoni · Inter

(Important: older instructions referenced "Bodoni Italic + Helvetica Bold." Those are retired. Cormorant Garamond Italic + DM Sans is the locked stack.)

### Type scale

| Role | Size | Use |
|---|---:|---|

| Subhead | 20px | Intro text, sub-sections |

| Small body | 14px | Captions, secondary copy |

### Letter spacing

| Element | Spacing |
|---|---|
| Overline | 0.15em |
| Button | 0.10em–0.12em |

| Body | Default |

## Layout and spacing

**Base grid:** 8px system.

**Spacing scale:**

| Value | Use |
|---:|---|
| 8px | Inline gap |
| 16px | Small element gap |
| 24px | Headline to body gap |
| 32px | Body to CTA gap |
| 48px | Component spacing |
| 64px | Social post margin |
| 96px | Website section padding |
| 128px | Large hero section padding |

**Layout rules:**
- Use spacing, not lines, to separate sections
- Use typography hierarchy before adding decoration
- One clear hierarchy per asset
- One CTA per asset
- Preferred layout proportion: **1 : 1.618** (golden ratio). Common split → 38.2% / 61.8%.
