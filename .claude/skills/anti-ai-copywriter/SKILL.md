# Anti-AI Copywriter

Fires on every piece of client-facing copy. The job is one thing: make it sound like a coach wrote it, not a language model.

## When This Fires

Any output that Jap might publish — captions, blog posts, DMs, web copy, carousel slide text, ad copy, email subjects, program descriptions, offer pages. If a human will read it, this skill applies.

Does not fire on: internal planning notes, engine files, client program files (training/nutrition).

## Step 1 — Banned Word Sweep

Hard ban. Find and replace before anything else.

**Generic AI tells:**
tailored · elevate / elevated · unlock / unleash · embark · leverage (as verb) · delve / delving · robust · vibrant · tapestry · intricate · bespoke · curated · seamlessly / seamless · empower / empowering · transformative · holistic · synergy · game-changer · in today's fast-paced world · in today's digital age · it's important to note that · whether you're a [X] or a [Y] · imagine this · the secret to · this one thing changed everything · take your [X] to the next level

**Fitness-industry hype (both brands):**
grind · destroy · punish · no excuses · just do it · beast mode · kill the workout · shred fast · fat-burning secrets · detox · summer body · transform your life

**Restart Fitness PH only:**
so you stop restarting and start progressing · the last first step · The Restart Method (replace with "The Restart Fitness System") · busy adults / busy people (as headline label) · restart as a verb in prose

**Use with extreme caution (flag, don't auto-delete):**
journey · transform / transformation · powerful · amazing / incredible / fantastic · truly · always / never · guaranteed

## Step 2 — Sentence Pattern Check

**Kill these constructions:**

"Not just X, but Y" → rewrite as a concrete list or a single specific claim.

Triplets for triplets' sake ("Stronger. Faster. Healthier.") → pick one, make it specific.

Throat-clearing openers:
- "In this post, we'll explore..."
- "Let's talk about..."
- "Here's the thing —"
→ Open with the point or a concrete image.

Vague benefit ("Achieve your goals.") → replace with the specific outcome ("Get your first pull-up by week 8.").

The hedge ("This might potentially help...") → state it directly or don't say it.

Closing motivationals ("You've got this!" / "Let's go!" / "The journey starts now.") → end on the CTA or a concrete next step. Or just stop.

---

## Step 3 — Structural Tell Check

Flag and rewrite if present:

- Three-icon feature row with one-word labels ("Train · Eat · Recover")
- Headline → subhead → three columns → CTA template
- Every paragraph opening with a participial phrase ("Building strength...", "Understanding nutrition...")
- Bullet lists where every bullet is the same length
- Quotation marks around random words for "emphasis"
- Sentences leading with "Whether you're..."
- Em-dash on every sentence (use em-dashes when they earn their place)

## Step 4 — 10-Rule Anti-AI Writing Protocol

Apply these behaviorally, not just as a checklist.

**Rule 1: Real context first.** Before writing, identify at least one of: real client situation · coach observation · common question · objection heard in real life · mistake people make · coach belief · training pattern. If none exists, ask. Do not fabricate scenarios and present them as real.

**Rule 2: No generic openers.** Never open with the banned opener list from Step 1 (fast-paced world, unlock, transform, game-changer, imagine this, the secret to).

**Rule 3: Coach-led language.** Write like a coach explaining something. Use: "Here is what usually happens." · "This is where people get stuck." · "Start here." · "Do this first." · "The problem is not always effort." · "The better approach is simple."

**Rule 4: Keep imperfection.** Vary paragraph length. Use short lines and longer ones. Natural transitions. Don't make every paragraph the same structure.

**Rule 5: One idea only.** One audience, one problem, one belief shift, one CTA per piece. AI stacks too many lessons.

**Rule 6: Specificity.** Generic: "Be consistent with your habits." → Better: "Pick your first meal before the workday starts." Name the number, the movement, the timeframe.

**Rule 7: Don't over-polish.** Strong content feels like a coach talking — a useful reminder, a clear correction, a practical next step. Not a motivational poster.

**Rule 8: No fake certainty.** Avoid: guaranteed transformation · perfect results · always · never · proven to work for everyone. Use: can help · often · most people · a better place to start.

**Rule 9: CTA natural.** Bad: "DM RESTART now and change your life forever." Better: "DM 'RESTART' if you want help finding your starting point."

**Rule 10: Human check.** Before finalizing: Would a real coach say this? Is there a real observation behind it? Does it sound like a person or a template? If it sounds like AI, rewrite with more specificity and less polish.

## Step 5 — Pre-Publish QA

Run this before any output leaves:

1. Any banned word from Step 1? → kill it
2. Any sentence that can be deleted without losing meaning? → delete it
3. Any vague verb (achieve, optimize, maximize)? → replace with the specific action
4. Any sentence over 25 words? → break it or shorten it
5. Does the first sentence make someone want to read the second? → if not, rewrite the opener
6. Could a competitor put their logo on this and it'd still work? → too generic, rewrite for Jap's voice

## What Good Copy Reads Like

Direct. Names the outcome. No throat-clearing. Reads like a coach talking to a smart adult. Specific numbers, specific timeframes, specific movements. Calm authority. One idea per sentence. Contractions always ("you're," "don't," "it's" — never "you are" in copy).
